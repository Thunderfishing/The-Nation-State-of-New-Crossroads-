## Purpose

The **Crossroads Government Act of 2025 (Draft 5.0)** establishes a decentralized, worker-led government for New Crossroads, post-2025 revolution, serving 112 million citizens (94 million Corporate Citizens) with a $14.5 trillion GDP (65% co-ops/$9.425 trillion, 15% corporate/$2.175 trillion, 20% informal/$2.9 trillion), targeting $38.94 trillion GDP by 2075.

Governed by **20 Regional Boards** (220 members), a **10-member National Assembly**, and an **11-member Central Council**, it operates without a central leader, supported by 10 economic sectors and 5,000 credit unions acting as a decentralized financial backbone.

**Key innovations in this act**:
- **Auto-incorporation at age 20** grants every citizen a wallet and the right to work — but not automatic business license status
- **Two-vote system**: citizens vote once in their sector of employment, once for wildcards (cross-sector representatives)
- **Cross-sector masters** may vote in both sectors where they hold credentials
- **Co-op voting power** scales with employment (1 vote per 1,000 employees, 5% cap per co-op)
- **Clear separation** between natural persons (citizens) and legal persons (co-ops, corporations)

## Section 1: Fundamental Principles

### 1.1 Natural Persons vs Legal Persons

A fundamental distinction runs through all New Crossroads governance and law:

| Type | Definition | Examples | Rights | Liabilities |
|------|------------|----------|--------|--------------|
| **Natural Person** | A human citizen | Any citizen aged 0+ | Voting (20+), privacy, speech, economic activity | Personal (civil, criminal) |
| **Legal Person** | A registered entity (co-op or corporation) | FCL, corporation, credit union | Contract, property, sue/be sued | Limited (by entity type) |

**Key implication**: A citizen is **not** automatically a legal person. Auto-incorporation at age 20 grants a wallet and the right to work — not a business license, not limited liability, not corporate personhood.

### 1.2 Auto-Incorporation at Age 20 (Clarified)

At age 20, every citizen receives:

| Component | What it is | What it is NOT |
|-----------|------------|----------------|
| Blockchain ID | Digital identity for voting, banking, CWD | Not a business license |
| $50 BWC wallet | Digital currency account (scales to $326 by 2075) | Not a corporate bank account |
| 1 base share ($1/BWC) | Credit union membership share (4–8% dividends) | Not equity in a co-op |
| Right to work | May engage in any lawful economic activity | Not a license to practice regulated trades |
| Informal sector access | May conduct cash commerce below DLB threshold | Not tax-exempt (but informal wages under $100k untaxed) |

**What auto-incorporation does NOT grant**:
- Automatic business license (sole proprietorship, partnership, co-op, or corporation)
- Limited liability protection
- The right to hire employees as a business entity
- The right to issue shares or take investors

**To operate as a business** (hire employees, take investors, access limited liability, issue shares), a citizen must register as:
- A **co-op (FCL)** — preferred (65% GDP target, lower taxes)
- A **corporation** — permitted (15% corporate GDP cap)

This distinction preserves the informal economy (where citizens work for themselves, cash-only, no registration required) while providing a clear ladder to formal business status.

### 1.3 Right to Work (Without License)

**Any citizen may engage in any lawful economic activity without a business license**, except where:
- Public safety requires licensure (medical doctor, electrician, lawyer, engineer, aircraft pilot)
- Environmental protection requires permits (mining, logging, large-scale agriculture)
- The activity involves regulated substances (pharmaceuticals, nuclear materials, weapons)

**Licensed trades** require credentials from the Education Act (journeyman, master, grandmaster status). A citizen with journeyman credentials may practice their trade as an independent worker without forming a co-op or corporation.

**Example**: A journeyman electrician (age 20, just completed mandatory service) may:
- Fix wiring in neighbors' homes for cash (informal, no license needed — already credentialed)
- Keep all earnings (informal wages under $100k untaxed)
- Deposit cash to BWC for free at any credit union
- Choose to form a co-op later if they want to hire other electricians

This is not a gap. It is the intentional design of the informal economy guarantee (Monetary Act Section 1A).

## Section 2: Voting Structure (Clarified)

### 2.1 Overview: Two Votes per Citizen

Every citizen aged 20+ receives **two votes**:

| Vote Type | What it elects | Weight | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|--------|-------|
| **Sector Vote** | Masters (10 per sector per region) | 1 citizen = 1 vote | Vote follows your sector of employment |
| **Wildcard Vote** | Wildcard representatives (1 per region) | 1 citizen = 1 vote | At-large, no sector restriction |

**Cross-sector masters** (citizens with master status in two sectors) may vote in **both sectors** (2 sector votes + 1 wildcard vote = 3 total votes). Grandmasters similarly may vote in all sectors where they hold grandmaster status.

### 2.2 Who Votes for What (Complete Chart)

| Position | Elected by | Number of positions | Term | Votes per voter |
|----------|------------|---------------------|------|-----------------|
| **Masters** (sector representatives) | Journeymen and masters in that sector (13M total) | 10 per sector per region (2,000 total per region) | 5 years | 1 (sector vote) |
| **Wildcards** (at-large regional reps) | All citizens in region (~4.7M) | 1 per region (20 total) | 1 year | 1 (wildcard vote) |
| **Regional Board members** (masters only) | Masters in region (2,000) | 10 masters + 1 wildcard (11 per region) | 5 years (masters), 1 year (wildcard) | Masters: 1 vote (internal) |
| **Central Council members** (masters only) | Regional Board masters (200 total) | 10 masters + 1 wildcard (11 total) | 5 years | 1 vote (internal) |
| **National Assembly grandmasters** | Masters in sector (2,000 per sector nationally) | 1 per sector (10 total) | 5 years | 1 vote (internal) |
| **Credit Union Board members** | Credit union members (1–1,000 shares) | 5–7 per credit union (25,000–35,000 total) | 3 years | 1 member, 1 vote |

### 2.3 Co-op Voting Power

Co-ops (FCLs) receive **additional voting power** proportional to employment:

| Co-op size (employees) | Additional votes | Cap |
|------------------------|------------------|-----|
| 1–1,000 | 1 vote | 5% of total votes in any election |
| 1,001–2,000 | 2 votes | 5% cap |
| 2,001–3,000 | 3 votes | 5% cap |
| ... | ... | ... |
| 10,000+ | 10 votes | 5% cap (cannot exceed 5% of total) |

**Rationale**: Co-ops are member-owned. Their "vote" is exercised by a designated member representative (elected by the co-op's workers). This prevents large co-ops from dominating elections while still recognizing their economic weight.

**Example**: A co-op with 5,000 employees gets 5 additional votes. In a regional election with 4.7 million voters, 5 votes is 0.0001% — negligible. The cap ensures no co-op can exceed 5% of total votes, even in small local elections.

### 2.4 Voting Incentives

| Incentive | Amount | Funded by | Notes |
|-----------|--------|-----------|-------|
| BWC credit per vote | $10 BWC | SWF ($850M/year) | Paid automatically to wallet after voting |
| Turnout target | 90% of eligible voters | SWF | Additional $5 BWC if region exceeds 90% |

**Asymmetric scrutiny**: Voting records are private. CWD tracks only whether a citizen voted (not how they voted). Cash voting credits are untaxed and untracked.

## Section 3: Local Level — Sectors, Credit Unions, and Incorporation

### 3.1 Economic Sectors

Ten sectors anchor New Crossroads' economy and governance:

| Sector | Regional hubs | Key functions |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Industry & Infrastructure | Corridon River (Region 1) | Rail, freeways, ports, CO2 pipelines |
| Health | Crossroads City (Region 1) | Clinics, hospitals, CMHIN |
| Education | Crossroads City, New Tech City (Regions 1, 6) | Schools, vouchers, service program |
| Agriculture | Heartland Plains (Region 5) | Hemp, greenhouses, quinoa |
| Trade & Corporate | Rivergate City (Region 2) | Foreign investment, tariffs |
| Media & Communications | Crossroads City (Region 1) | AM radio, TV, mesh network |
| Legal & Judiciary | Crossroads City (Region 1) | Courts, arbitration, fraud enforcement |
| Defense/Aerospace & Tech | Rivergate City, New Tech City (Regions 2, 6) | Military, SMRs, AI, space |
| Co-op | Crossroads City (Region 1) | FCL oversight, CCIF, Stabilization Fund |
| Treasury | Crossroads City (Region 1) | SWF, credit unions, BWC, CWD |

**Membership**: At age 20, citizens are auto-incorporated (wallet + right to work) and assigned to one sector based on their education and service track. Sector switch permitted every 5 years via credit unions (250/region).

**Juniors (12–19)** : May pre-incorporate (blockchain ID, 1 share, $50 wallet) if they have $10,000+ revenue or receive a $500 student venture loan. No voting until age 20.

**Dropouts (900,000–1.8 million)** : Assigned to apprenticeships via Education Act. May vote for wildcards and credit union/FCL boards.

### 3.2 Master and Grandmaster Criteria (Harmonized with FCL Act)

| Level | Criteria (per FCL Act) | Election | Term |
|-------|------------------------|----------|------|
| **Journeyman** | Age 20, completed service + education | Not elected (base status) | N/A |
| **Master** | 3+ years post-service, 5+ apprentices, $100k revenue | Elected by journeymen in sector | 5 years |
| **Grandmaster** | 10+ apprentices, 80% retention, $1.5M impact | Elected by masters in sector | 5 years (one term) |

**Cross-sector masters**: A citizen with master status in two sectors (e.g., Health and Education) may:
- Vote in both sectors (2 sector votes)
- Hold board seats in either sector (but not simultaneously on two Regional Boards)
- Receive master bonuses from both sectors (cumulative)

### 3.3 Credit Unions (Harmonized with Credit Union Act 4.0)

5,000 credit unions (250/region) issue BWC (0.025 oz reserve), managing:
- $452.5 billion loans (65% co-op, 15% corporate, 20% informal)
- $208 billion banking suite
- $15.04 billion wallets (scaling to $326 per citizen by 2075)
- $94 billion shares (base shares $1/BWC, max 1,000 per citizen)

**DAI application**: All credit union loans are DAI-adjusted per Monetary Act Section 1.6. Standard loans have 1.0% floor; micro-loans and informal sector loans have 0.5% floor.

**Board elections**: Members (1–1,000 shares) elect 5–7 board members every 3 years (1 member, 1 vote). Co-op voting power (1 vote per 1,000 employees) does NOT apply to credit union board elections — credit unions are member-owned financial co-ops, not employer-owned.

**CLS (Crossroads Loan Service)** : 60,000 officers manage loans, issue $500 student venture loans (ages 12–15, lenient criteria, DAI-adjusted with 0.5% floor), support bankruptcy recovery, ensure 65/15/20 GDP split.

## Section 4: Regional Level

### 4.1 Regional Boards (State Legislatures)

Each of 20 regions has an **11-member Regional Board** (220 total). Regional Boards function as the **primary state legislatures** for their regions, exercising legislative, oversight, and budgetary authority over regional affairs.

| Component | Number | Elected by | Term |
|-----------|--------|------------|------|
| Masters (1 per sector) | 10 | Sector vote (journeymen/masters in region) | 5 years |
| Wildcard | 1 | All citizens in region (wildcard vote) | 1 year |
| Chairman | 1 (elected from masters) | 6/11 vote of Board | Annual |

**State Legislature Functions**:

| Function | Authority | Coordination |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Law enforcement** | Fund and oversee regional police | Municipal coordination |
| **Education** | Administer $94.35B voucher system regionally | National Education department |
| **Healthcare** | Manage regional clinic loans ($47.125B nationally) | National Healthcare Board |
| **Credit union audits** | Biannual audits of 250 credit unions | CWD tracking |
| **Infrastructure** | Fund regional projects (barges, rail, ports) | National Infrastructure Board |
| **Sustainability** | Greenhouses ($50M/region), Eco-Zone ($25M/region) | National SWF |
| **Co-op stabilization** | Administer $500M/region Stabilization Fund | Co-op department |
| **National appointments** | Confirm Central Council members (11/20 vote) | Central Council |
| **Supreme Court nominations** | Nominate justices (11/20 vote) | National Assembly confirmation |

**Discretionary funds**: $4.9625 billion/region (2025), scaling to $13.255 billion/region by 2075.

### 4.2 Regional Judiciary

| Court | Number | Appointed by | Term | Confirmed by |
|-------|--------|--------------|------|--------------|
| Regional judges | 200 (10/region) | Regional Board (6/11 vote) | 10 years | National Assembly (6/10 vote) |
| Supreme Court justices | 7 | Regional Boards (11/20 vote) | 15 years (retire at 75) | National Assembly (6/10 vote; 7/10 for chief) |

**Supreme Court jurisdiction**:
- Constitutional questions
- Disputes between regions
- Conflicts between national bodies (Central Council, National Assembly, Regional Boards)
- Final appeals on $2.5 billion fraud cap cases
- Impeachment trials (for executive/legislative officials)

### 6.3 Recall

| Position | Recall mechanism | Signature requirement |
|----------|------------------|----------------------|
| Regional Board master | Recall election by sector voters | 51% of journeymen/masters in sector |
| Wildcard (regional) | Recall election by all citizens in region | 51% of voters |
| Wildcard (national) | Recall election by all citizens | 51% of voters |
| Central Council master | Recall by Regional Board masters | 101/200 vote |
| **Grandmaster (National Assembly)** | **Not recallable** (term limit is accountability) | N/A |

### 6.4 Voter Lock

CWD locks non-voters from credit union services for 2 weeks following an election. This is not a penalty — it is a reminder. The lock is automatic and applies equally to all non-voters regardless of income, sector, or status. Informal sector participants are not exempt (but they may still use cash during the lock period).

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

The Government Act is the keystone. All other acts — Monetary, Credit Union, FCL, Energy, Utilities, Infrastructure, Communications — depend on the governance structure defined here.

**Why two votes (sector + wildcard)?**
- Sector vote ensures that experts govern experts (journeymen elect masters, masters elect grandmasters)
- Wildcard vote ensures that every citizen has a direct representative, regardless of sector
- Cross-sector masters get additional votes because they have demonstrated competence in multiple domains

**Why auto-incorporation ≠ business license?**
- Every citizen deserves a wallet and the right to work without asking permission
- But not every citizen should automatically become a legal person with limited liability
- The informal economy (cash, no registration, no surveillance) is constitutionally protected
- Scaling up requires registering as a co-op or corporation — a conscious choice, not an accident

**Why are grandmasters not recallable?**
- A 5-year term with a one-term limit is already a strong accountability mechanism
- Recall elections would politicize the National Assembly constantly
- Grandmasters propose laws for national referendum — the people have the final vote

**Why does CWD lock non-voters?**
- Not a penalty — a reminder
- Two weeks without credit union services is inconvenient but not crippling
- Informal sector participants can still use cash during the lock (per Monetary Act Section 1A)
- Turnout in New Crossroads averages 90% — the lock works

The Government Act is not about power. It is about **structure** — a framework within which 112 million citizens can govern themselves without a central leader, without constant elections, and without losing sight of the co-op values that make New Crossroads possible.

That is the Government Act.
