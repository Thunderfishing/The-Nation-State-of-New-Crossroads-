## 2. Structural Composition — The Three-Sector Model

New Crossroads is not a conventional mixed economy. Output is split across three structurally distinct sectors, held at the **65/15/20** ratio across the founding Acts and the Geography document:

| Sector | Share | 2010 value | 2060 value | Character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Cooperative** | 65% | $9.425T | $25.311T | Worker-owned FCLs, credit unions, co-op clubs; 5% profit tax; patronage returns |
| **Corporate** | 15% | $2.175T | $5.841T | Heavy-industry-clause firms, foreign-investment CCJVs; 15–20% profit tax |
| **Informal** | 20% | $2.9T | $7.788T | Cash commerce below the Daily Living Basket; constitutionally protected, untracked, untaxed to ~$100k |

This is the system's signature: a **cooperative-dominant** productive economy, a disciplined corporate sector for capital-intensive heavy industry, and a large protected informal economy that functions as a social shock-absorber, entrepreneurial nursery, and bankruptcy-recovery zone.

Its modest share is a feature, not an underdevelopment.

**Leading export categories:** potash & fertilizer (potash + green ammonia), platinum-group metals & uranium, synthetic fuel & green hydrogen, green steel & aluminum, heavy mining equipment, manufactured goods (EVs, ships, transformers, electronics), premium timber & hemp products, jewelry, carbon-removal credits, and technology/consultancy services (SMRs, atmospheric management, privacy & monetary architecture).

**Why imports stay low:** systematic import-substitution (domestic SMRs, transformers, cable, steel, equipment, fuel, fertilizer) plus the resource base means the nation buys mainly specialty inputs and select consumer goods. Combined with the export base, this produces a durable surplus and a steadily rising stock of foreign assets — funding the SWF and reinforcing the metal-backed currency.

## 9. Fiscal & Monetary Position

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **National debt** | **$0** — no central bank, no quantitative easing, no fiat creation, no sovereign borrowing |
| **Bonds** | Constitutionally permitted; never issued in 50 years |
| **Money supply** | Dual: physical cash (velocity, metal-embedded, surveillance-protected) + BWC (blockchain store-of-value, deflationary) |
| **Lending** | Only from real prior savings (credit-union special shares) — no money creation by lending |
| **Sovereign Wealth Fund** | Capped at 12% of GDP (~$4.7T ceiling); surplus → equal citizen dividends |
| **Debt jubilees** | 25-year cycles (2035, 2060) as structural pressure-release |
| **Consumer credit** | No credit cards; micro-loans + BNPL only → no consumer-debt spiral |

**The headline:** A **$38.94 trillion** economy carrying **zero national debt**, running a persistent trade surplus, holding a metal-backed currency that has appreciated ~3.6× in real terms over 50 years, with no banking crisis in its history. The fiscal position is not "low debt." It is **no debt**, by constitutional design.
