# Crossroads Sovereign Remainder Act of 2025: Draft 0.3

Posted to r/Bulwarkomics
Draft: 0.3 | Date: July 4, 2026
Author: Thunderfish2

**Cross-reference.** Companion to the Chamber Act (the constituent layers — the credit-union territorial chamber, the co-op and corporate-labor productive chambers, and the supreme universal citizen vote — referenced throughout; its §11 holds the shared entrenched-floors schedule), the Monetary Act 9.6 (issuance anchor §1.5, deficit-financing bar, crisis framework §1.8, PPK/CED), the Credit Union Act 5.6 (RLP/DIP, the credit-union base layer), the Exchange Act 1.1 (Global Sound-Capital Segment §7 — statecraft powers relocated here), the Municipal & Regional Funding Act 1.1 (MMF/RMF; SWF as cross-regional lender of last resort), the FCL Act 3.2 (CCIF as the co-op sector's own fund, distinct from the SWF), the Military & Intelligence Act 1.7 (command, the Defense Fund, CMIS, the arms-export veto), and the Infrastructure Act 2.5 and Industry Act 4.3 (the takings power and the resource-revenue basis).

**This act is deliberately separate from the Chamber Act.** The two are not two halves of one constitution. The remainder is the *residue* left after everything automatable was automated and everything mutualizable was mutualized; the chambers and the citizens are the *source* of authority the remainder answers to. Fusing them into a single charter would imply a co-equality the whole design denies. They travel together and are ratified together; they are not one document.

**Note on figures.** Illustrative projections from a 2025 base; the design is in the structure and ratios, not the precise values.

## Amendment note — Draft 0.3

Draft 0.3 keeps every 0.2 provision and makes three additions, one reference fix, and two rejections.

**Kept separate.** The proposal to combine this act and the Chamber Act into one "Constitutional Acts" document is **rejected**. The remainder must read as the smaller, enumerated, subordinate thing — not as Part I of a charter with the legislature as Part II. Two acts, ratified together, never merged.

**Additions folded in (procedural tightening from the external review, accepted on the merits):**
- **§1.4 — Citizen standing with cost-shifting.** Universal standing to challenge any remainder act, with costs awardable to a prevailing petitioner — access preserved, frivolity discouraged.
- **§2.2 — The treaty-limbo rule.** A treaty binding domestic law takes no effect until the Supreme Court rules on its consistency, or 180 days pass without a petition. No treaty slips into force before review.
- **§2.4 — Citizen oversight of foreign-listing suspensions.** A 1% petition forces a public justification for any suspension of a foreign listing longer than 90 days and triggers Territorial-Chamber review; the SAP adjudicates any claim of necessary secrecy. The one statecraft power that had no democratic check now has one.

**Reference fix:**
- **§8.9** now points at the Chamber §11 entrenched-floor schedule **as a whole**, rather than enumerating a subset, so the list cannot drift between the two acts.

**Rejections (recorded on the record):**
- **No emergency suspension of the entrenched floors.** A proposal to permit temporary suspension of certain floors in a "declared existential emergency" is rejected. The constitution's core promise — stated here in §8 and in the Chamber §11 — is that *no war, emergency, or treaty pierces the floors.* A pre-authorized suspension door is more dangerous than any scenario it anticipates. The floors are absolute. The only standing emergency power, Tier-2 mode (§5.3), is economic and touches no right.

## Abstract

Five acts — Monetary, Credit Union, Exchange, Municipal & Regional, FCL — govern nearly the whole of New Crossroads life through one repeated machine: published rules in place of official discretion, peer mutual pools in place of central budgets, and independent audit in place of trust. Money is issued by rule. Credit is priced by rule. Crises are met by pre-committed trigger. Regions insure regions; cities insure cities; co-ops fund their own capital pool. The economy, in short, governs itself.

This act establishes what remains — the sovereign remainder: the short list of functions that are irreducibly discretionary, irreducibly political, and irreducibly singular, which no rule can automate, no mutual can pool, and no chamber can neutrally hold. There are four: the defense of the nation and its posture toward other nations; the independent prosecution of anyone, including the state itself; the judgment of disputes, including disputes between the constituent layers; and the keeping of the sovereign fund of last resort. The remainder holds these four functions, is constitutionally barred from every other, is staffed from below, recallable from below, funded without the printer, and watched by its own prosecutors.

The remainder is thin because the rest of the system is thick. It is not a small government trying to stay small; it is the residue left after everything automatable was automated and everything mutualizable was mutualized. Its size is not a policy — it is a remainder.

## Section 1: The Enumeration Principle

*Rationale:* every state grows by discovering new problems only it can solve. The remainder is built to make that discovery impossible: it holds an enumerated list, the list is closed, and the burden of proof is inverted — a function belongs to the remainder only if it can be shown that no rule, mutual, chamber, council, or citizen vote can hold it. Doubt resolves downward, always.

### 1.1 The Four Functions

The sovereign remainder holds exactly four functions:

1. **Defense & Foreign Affairs** (Section 2) — the military, treaties, customs and the border, and the statecraft powers of the Exchange's global segment.
2. **The Special Auditors** (Section 3) — independent, self-initiating prosecution of any entity's books, including the remainder's own.
3. **The Judiciary** (Section 4) — the courts, the Supreme Court, the Special Arbiter Panel, the warranted Judicial Link Box, and the judicial ratification of the delegated takings power (§4.4).
4. **The Sovereign Wealth Fund** (Section 5) — the fund of last resort, its automatic stabilizers, and the Defense Fund endowment.

### 1.2 The Downward Presumption

Any function not enumerated above belongs, in order of preference, to: a published rule; a mutual pool; a self-governing chamber; a municipal council; or the universal citizen vote — and never to the remainder. Where a new function arises and its home is disputed, the Special Arbiter Panel assigns it, and the SAP is bound by the same downward presumption: the remainder receives a function only on a showing that every lower home is structurally impossible, not merely inconvenient.

### 1.3 The Closed List

Adding a function to Section 1.1 requires the highest amendment bar in the constitutional order (Chamber Act §10.5): supermajorities of every chamber — territorial and both productive — plus a 75% national referendum. Removing a function from the list — pushing it down to a rule, mutual, or chamber — requires only the ordinary amendment procedure. It is deliberately easier to shrink the remainder than to grow it.

### 1.4 Citizen Standing

Any citizen may petition the Supreme Court to review any act of the remainder for consistency with this Section. Standing is deliberately universal. The Court may award costs to a prevailing petitioner, discouraging frivolous filings without gating access to judicial review.

## Section 2: Defense & Foreign Affairs

*Rationale:* war and diplomacy are the canonical sovereign acts — judgments about the nation's posture toward other nations, made under uncertainty, with no formula that can carry them. They are also the functions most dangerous to leave lying around: a defense establishment with a domestic mission becomes a domestic power. This section therefore does two things at once — it concentrates the outward-facing powers in the remainder, and it severs every inward-facing tendril those powers might grow.

### 2.1 The Military

- **Command:** Civilian command of the standing professional force (~125K + CMIS) vests in the Sovereign Council (Section 6). Operational command in a professional staff per the Military Act 1.7. The Council does NOT command the 9.1M regional militia — that force answers to the Territorial Chamber (Chamber Act §3.3), keeping the mass of the nation's arms decentralized and out of the central executive, in peace and in war.
- **Funding:** The recurring operating budget (~$40B/year, declining) is paid from the Defense Fund endowment as it matures (Monetary §2.5) and from general sovereign revenue (Section 7) in the interim. Never from issuance; never from the SWF principal.
- **Domestic bar:** The military has no domestic policing, surveillance, or enforcement role. It may not be deployed against citizens, may not access the CED, and holds no jurisdiction inside the border except on declared invasion of the national territory. Its only permitted domestic presence is the school liaison-and-recruitment seat (Chamber Act §8.3), which confers no command, surveillance, or enforcement role.
- **The declaration of war:** The Sovereign Council may act defensively at once against a declared invasion of the national territory (standing force only; militia muster per Chamber §3.3). Offensive war or force projection requires ratification by the Territorial Chamber at the 15/20 supermajority within 30 days, absent which forces stand down. All war authority carries the Wartime-Fee pattern — hard sunset, forced re-authorization (Monetary §1.3). War is ratified by the territorial chamber, not the productive chambers: it is a matter of the nation as places.

### 2.1a The CMIS Anti-Surveillance Wall

*Rationale:* the single most likely place the sword turns inward is not through tanks, through packets. CMIS runs the cyber division that defends the national banking suite and the satellite backbone. Defending the banking rails is a hair's breadth from watching who transacts on them, so the wall must be architectural, not merely a policy.

- CMIS may defend national infrastructure — the banking suite, the grid controls, the comms backbone — against external attack, but may never surveil the population, may never access the PPK beyond its own personnel records, and may never reach the CED.
- Cyber-defense capability must be architecturally separated from any capability to monitor domestic transactions: the systems that harden the rails and the systems that could read them are not the same systems, and CMIS holds only the former.
- CMIS conducts no deep espionage and no proactive foreign engagement, and any inward-facing capability is void (Section 8).

### 2.2 Foreign Affairs

- **Treaties:** Negotiated by the Sovereign Council; ratified by both chambers jointly, with a universal-citizen-vote backstop for any treaty that would bind domestic law.
- **The treaty-limbo rule (new).** Whether a treaty "binds domestic law" is a constitutional question reviewable by the Supreme Court on any citizen's petition, with standing deliberately universal. A treaty binding domestic law **shall not take effect until the Court issues a ruling, or the 180th day after ratification passes without a petition having been filed.** No treaty slips into force ahead of review.
- A treaty may not do domestically what the remainder is barred from doing domestically (Section 8) or breach any entrenched floor (Chamber §11). No treaty may create a surveillance obligation, a monetary obligation on the printer, a tax or taking on homes beyond the §4.4 rule, or a subordination of the Informal Economy Guarantee. A treaty inconsistent with these is void as domestic law, reviewable by the Supreme Court.

### 2.3 Customs & the Border

- The remainder administers the border: tariffs (~9.8% avg, ~$49B/year), the cash-export prohibition (Monetary §1.1), and customs enforcement at ports and crossings.
- The arms-export veto, rewired: a national-security finding is a Sovereign Council statecraft function (foreign-facing), and the finding compels a suspension of the export; the override moves to the chambers — the elected bodies get the last word, on the standard veto-override terms. The principle survives intact; only the wiring changes, and it fits better here, because the Council is the foreign-affairs organ.
- Tariff schedules are set by the chambers and administered — not set — by the remainder. The remainder collects; it does not decide.

### 2.4 The Global Sound-Capital Segment (statecraft powers)

- The Exchange Act §7 statecraft powers relocate here: accrediting the segment's auditors, approving and suspending foreign listings on foreign-policy or national-security grounds, and the phase-two activation determination.
- The domestic Exchange remains entirely outside the remainder — a thin utility under its own act, stewarded by the cooperative bodies. Only the foreign-facing segment, being statecraft, lands here.
- **Citizen oversight of foreign listings (new).** Any suspension or bar of a foreign listing is subject to a citizen-initiated review. A petition of **1% of national voters** may compel the Sovereign Council to publish a public justification for any suspension or bar of more than **90 days'** duration, and may trigger a Territorial Chamber review (15/20 to uphold, override, or refer to the SAP). The power to suspend is sovereign; the power to review is democratic. No suspension may be kept secret from the citizenry beyond the minimum necessary to protect national security, as determined by the SAP on the petition of any citizen or listed issuer.

## Section 3: The Special Auditors (Independent Special Prosecutors)

*Rationale:* the one institution that cannot be a mutual, a rule, or a chamber is the one that must be able to indict all of them. An auditor owned by the chambers cannot audit the chambers; an auditor on a formula can be gamed by whoever writes the inputs. Independence here is not a preference — it is the function.

### 3.1 Composition & Appointment

- 50–75 auditors, 5-year terms.
- Appointment: nominated by the Sovereign Council, confirmed by the Territorial Chamber (Regional Boards — the broadest, least-concentrated membership base).
- **Citizen backstop:** Because the auditors are the officials most able to abuse ordinary citizens and citizens the least able to lobby them, auditor confirmations are subject to a universal-vote veto — a national petition (at the Chamber §6.3 threshold) can force any auditor confirmation to a ratifying referendum. The prosecutor who can charge anyone answers, ultimately, to everyone.

### 3.2 Independence

- Removable only by joint action of the Sovereign Council and the Territorial Chamber (the confirming layer) — no single official, department, chamber, or body may fire, defund, or quash an auditor investigating it.
- Budget constitutionally protected; may not be cut mid-term; set as a fixed formula share of sovereign revenue (Section 7) so no annual appropriation can starve an inconvenient investigation.
- An official or body under investigation may not participate in any decision affecting that auditor.

### 3.3 Jurisdiction & Powers

- **Self-initiating:** an auditor may open an investigation into any entity's books — any co-op, corporation, credit union, chamber, mutual fund, municipal council, sovereign organ, or officer of the remainder itself — on their own initiative, without authorization from anyone.
- Subpoena documents, records, financial statements, and testimony.
- Bring charges before the regular judiciary (regional courts → Supreme Court on appeal). The auditors prosecute; they never judge.
- Temporarily freeze assets pending investigation — ratified by a regional judge within 14 days or the freeze lapses automatically.
- Enforcement surface inherited from the sibling acts: Exchange violations, Municipal Act gaming and below-cost deals, Informal Sector Mandate violations, CED fraud (~$2.5B cap), and the global segment's audit-to-standard.

### 3.4 Limits (absolute)

- May not access cash transaction records below the DLB threshold (Informal Economy Guarantee).
- May not compel how a citizen voted (PPK-protected).
- Operate through the CED (pseudonymized); identity reached only via the warranted Judicial Link Box.
- Charges tried in the independent judiciary, never by the auditors themselves.

*(The press floor and the due-process floor of Chamber §11 stand over this office specifically: a free press is the external check on a self-initiating prosecutor, and no charge convicts anyone but the judiciary.)*

## Section 4: The Judiciary

*Rationale:* every power in this act is checked by a judge — the auditor's freeze (14 days), the Link Box (warrant), the treaty power (Section 8 review), the emergency mode (sunset review). The judiciary is therefore the load-bearing organ of the remainder: it is what makes the other three functions safe to hold. It also houses the one arbitration body no chamber can hold — the Special Arbiter Panel — because a dispute between chambers cannot be judged by a chamber.

### 4.1 Structure

- **Regional judges:** 200 (10/region), 10-year terms. Appointed by the Territorial Chamber (Regional Boards, 6/11), confirmed by the Productive Chambers (6/10 equivalent).
- **Supreme Court:** 7 justices, 15-year terms, mandatory retirement at 75, decisions by 5/7. Nominated by the Territorial Chamber and confirmed by the Productive Chambers (7/10 equivalent for the chief justice). Hears constitutional questions, inter-regional disputes, conflicts between chambers and between chambers and the remainder, final fraud-cap appeals, takings-power ratification appeals (§4.4), and Section 8 / entrenched-floor review.
- **The deadlock rule:** If a nominating body's first 3 nominees for a seat are each rejected, the confirming body must confirm one of those 3 within 1 week of the third rejection — no seat sits vacant on repeated rejection.

### 4.2 The Special Arbiter Panel (SAP)

- 9 arbiters, 3 panels of 3, 5-year terms. Appointment: nominated by the Supreme Court, confirmed by the Territorial Chamber — a judicial nomination (to keep the SAP inside the judicial family) with a territorial-chamber check (so it is not wholly self-appointing).
- Deadlock referral is mandatory binding arbitration — parties do not opt in and cannot decline — resolved by a panel randomly assigned per case.
- **Jurisdiction (consolidated):** disputes between a regulator and its paired body; MMF/RMF conditionality and contributor-standing appeals; Exchange enforcement appeals; CCJV and Co-op Department arbitrations; disputes between chambers; Section 1.2 function-assignment disputes (bound by the downward presumption); and the floor-classification questions the Chamber Act assigns it (whether a citizen-initiated law touches a floor, §6.3; whether a floor amendment is clarificatory or substantive, §10.5).
- **Composition rule:** each panel includes at least one arbiter with cross-layer standing — defined as membership across two economic forms (e.g., active in both a credit union and a Tier-2+ co-op board), NOT the holding of two grandmaster credentials. Standing comes from where you belong, not what you have mastered.

### 4.3 The Judicial Link Box

- The only bridge between PPK identity and CED economic data: warrant on probable cause, signed by a regional judge, two-region approval, publicly logged, citizen notified within 90 days, annual abuse audit.
- Housed in the judiciary, operated by no one else, and constitutionally incapable of bulk operation: the Link Box maps one hash to one identity per warrant. A warrant class, a standing warrant, or a programmatic interface to the Link Box is void.
- It is the single warranted door behind the privacy floors (Chamber §11): financial privacy, media-consumption privacy, housing-payment privacy, vote privacy, and search of person and data all reach identity only here.

### 4.4 The Takings Power (Eminent Domain) and Its Judicial Check

*Rationale:* the infrastructure acts imply the state building linear assets across land it does not own — 75,000 km of rail, 15,000 km of freeway, pipelines, dredged channels. That is a compulsory-acquisition power, and no act granted or limited it. It is a sovereign attribute (only a sovereign can compel a sale), but it is dangerous precisely where the system is most protective — the home. So the power is granted narrowly, its exercise delegated to the infrastructure authorities, and it is consummated only by the judiciary.

- The power exists but is bounded: a taking is permitted only for genuine public infrastructure, never for economic development and never for transfer to another private party.
- Compensation is a fixed rule, not a discretionary appraisal: market value + 10%. The premium makes the compelled owner more than whole for an involuntary sale — consistent with the rule that the citizen never loses by the state's action.
- Exercise is delegated, not central: the relevant infrastructure authority acts — the National Infrastructure Board for national works, the municipal council for local roads and utilities. The Sovereign Council holds no general takings power of its own.
- **Judicial ratification is mandatory:** no title transfers until a judge confirms both the genuine public-infrastructure purpose and the market-plus-10% valuation. This mirrors the auditor-freeze pattern — the state may act, but a judge must sign — and appeals run to the Supreme Court (§4.1).
- The primary residence is not exempt from the power (a freeway must sometimes cross a parcel) but is protected by it: the public-infrastructure-only bar, the no-private-transfer bar, the premium, and the judicial check are the home's guarantee, completing the three-part home doctrine (never taxed / never arbitrarily taken / never surveilled — Chamber §11).

## Section 5: The Sovereign Wealth Fund

*Rationale:* three acts have circled the same question — who owns the fund of last resort. The answer: the remainder holds it, because lender-of-last-resort is a sovereign act. "Should the whole system be saved, and at what price" is not a question a formula can answer or a mutual can pool — the mutuals (RLP, DIP, MMF, RMF, CCIF) are the system; the SWF is what stands behind the system when the system itself is the thing failing. But holding is not spending: the SWF's ordinary operations are already automated by rule, and this act keeps them that way. The remainder is the fund's keeper, not its discretionary spender.

### 5.1 What the Remainder Holds

- The SWF (~$550B → ~$1.5–2.0T, capped at 12% of GDP) and the Defense Fund endowment (separate, ~$920B at maturity).
- Distinction preserved: the CCIF (~$2.395T) is the co-op sector's own voluntary pool, chamber-governed, and is not a sovereign asset. The Citizens' Metals ETF backs its shareholders. The sovereign metals reserve is a strategic asset of the remainder, never listed, never traded.

### 5.2 What Remains Automatic (rules, not the remainder)

- The 12% cap, the automatic citizen dividend above it, and the contribution cuts — untouched, automatic.
- The Tier-1 crisis stabilizers: on an objective systemic-freeze trigger, the RLP→SWF line opens automatically at reference + 2%. No sovereign vote occurs; the trigger is the authorization. The remainder may not close the line while the trigger condition holds.
- MMF/RMF lender-of-last-resort access: on cross-regional emergency certification — the certification gate retained, with the certifying bodies re-mapped by the Chamber Act.

### 5.3 What Is Discretionary (and therefore sovereign)

- Tier-2 emergency mode: for the genuinely novel event no rule foresaw — activated by Sovereign Council supermajority (5/7) plus Territorial Chamber concurrence (15/20 Regional Boards, mirroring the war and militia-muster standard), hard sunsets, forced re-authorization, every action published. This is the one standing discretionary economic power in the constitutional order, and it is time-boxed by construction. **Its scope is economic; it touches no entrenched floor and no political right (§8).**
- Extraordinary SWF deployment within a declared Tier-2 mode, each deployment carrying its own sunset.

### 5.4 The Bars

- The SWF may never fund the remainder's ordinary operations (that is Section 7's job) — a fund that pays its keeper's salary is a fund the keeper will grow.
- The deficit-financing bar binds the remainder absolutely: no issuance ever funds the remainder, in peace, crisis, or war. War is funded by the Defense Fund, sovereign revenue, and — on the Wartime-Fee pattern — time-boxed fees, never the printer.

## Section 6: The Sovereign Council

*Rationale:* four functions need an executive: someone must command the military, conduct diplomacy, nominate auditors and justices, and turn the Tier-2 key. This section creates the smallest body that can do those things and nothing else. It is deliberately not a government: it has no departments, no domestic programs, no budget beyond Section 7, and no agenda-setting power over the chambers.

### 6.1 Composition

- 7 members, 5-year terms, one-term limit: 6 professional/executive seats + 1 universal wildcard. Five is too thin for a body that commands the military and nominates auditors and justices (recusal risks tie deadlock); nine begins to resemble a government, which this body must never become. Seven gives 5/7 supermajority mechanics matching the Supreme Court; the single wildcard seat means the thin state is never wholly chosen by institutions.
- Chairman elected annually by the Council from among its members; represents the remainder; casts no extra vote.

### 6.2 Selection & Recall

- **Selection:** the 6 professional seats are nominated by the Territorial Chamber and confirmed by the Productive Chambers jointly; the 1 wildcard seat is elected directly by the universal citizen vote.
- **Recall:** every electing body may recall the member(s) it elected by 2/3 supermajority — the professional seats by their nominating/confirming chambers, the wildcard by the universal vote.
- **Impeachment:** for treason, bribery, corruption, or gross neglect — impeached by the chambers (7/10 equivalent), tried before the Supreme Court.

### 6.3 Powers (closed list)

- Command the military (§2.1); negotiate treaties (§2.2); administer customs (§2.3); exercise the global-segment statecraft powers (§2.4).
- Nominate Special Auditors (§3.1) and, per §4.1, participate in judicial appointments as defined.
- Turn the Tier-2 key with chamber concurrence (§5.3).
- Nothing else. The Council has no veto over chamber legislation, no appointment power into any chamber, mutual, credit union, municipal council, or the domestic Exchange, and no emergency power beyond §5.3's time-boxed mode.

## Section 7: Funding the Remainder

*Rationale:* a sovereign that can print funds itself forever; a sovereign that must ask its citizens annually stays thin. The remainder lives on a narrow, published revenue base, and its total operating envelope is constitutionally capped.

- **Revenue:** three flows, each already national by nature — tariffs at the border (~$49B); the national resource cut (the 3% surcharge + resource-firm profit tax, ~$80–100B), noting that the minerals themselves are regionally owned and the royalties are regional own-source revenue (the national layer takes only the surcharge + profit tax, never the royalty); and the corporate SWF contribution stream. No flow taxes citizens, homes, co-ops, or the informal economy. The remainder is funded by the border, the subsoil cut, and corporate discipline — nothing else.
- **The operating cap:** the remainder's total operating spending (military operations, auditors, judiciary, Council, customs administration) may not exceed 4% of GDP, declining toward ~2% as the Defense Fund endowment matures to cover military operations (~$40B → ~$0 net by ~2070). The cap is constitutional; breaching it requires the middle amendment bar (Chamber §10.5).
- Surplus above operations flows to the SWF (within its 12% cap) or, above that, to the automatic citizen dividend — the remainder cannot accumulate.
- No issuance (§5.4), no sovereign debt (there are no bonds to issue), no SWF-principal draw for operations.

## Section 8: The Prohibitions (What the Remainder May Never Do)

*Rationale:* Section 1 says what the remainder holds; this section makes the complement explicit, because the great constitutional failures are failures of implication — powers assumed because they were not denied. Each prohibition below is already law; gathering them here binds them onto the sovereign specifically, so no emergency, war, or treaty can pierce them.

The remainder may never:

1. Create money, direct its creation, or fund itself by issuance — the two-taps rule stands; the remainder is neither tap.
2. Tax residential land or the primary home; or take a home except under the bounded §4.4 takings power — public infrastructure only, market-plus-10%, judicially ratified, never for private-to-private transfer.
3. Surveil the citizen: no access to sub-threshold cash life (Informal Economy Guarantee), no PPK/CED merger outside the Link Box, no bulk warrant, no domestic military or intelligence surveillance mission.
4. Turn CMIS inward: CMIS may defend national infrastructure but may never surveil the population, access the PPK beyond its own personnel records, or reach the CED. Cyber-defense capability is architecturally separated from any capability to monitor domestic transactions (§2.1a). Any inward-facing CMIS capability is void.
5. Regulate the domestic economy: prices, rates, entry, licensure, lending standards, listing standards, and sector policy belong to the rules, mutuals, chambers, and councils that hold them now.
6. Appoint into, veto, or dissolve any chamber, credit union, mutual fund, municipal council, or the domestic Exchange.
7. Carry sovereign debt or issue bonds.
8. Suspend elections, recalls, the citizen vote, or the Informal Economy Guarantee — including under Tier-2 emergency mode, whose scope is economic and which touches no political right.
9. **Breach any entrenched floor in the shared schedule (Chamber Act §11).** The schedule is referenced here as a whole — not enumerated in part — so it cannot drift between the two acts. Every floor in it binds the remainder and the chambers alike; **no war, emergency, or treaty pierces any of them.** There is no emergency-suspension exception; the only standing emergency power is the economic Tier-2 mode (§5.3), which touches no floor.
10. Hold any function not enumerated in Section 1.1.

A sovereign act inconsistent with this section is void, reviewable by the Supreme Court on any citizen's petition, with standing deliberately universal.

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

New Crossroads discovered, act by act, that almost nothing a government does requires a government. Money did not — a rule issues it. Credit did not — mutuals price it. Crises did not — triggers meet them. Equalization did not — peers insure peers. Markets did not — a rulebook and its stewards run them. What was left, when the discovering was done, was short: someone must face the world, someone must be able to indict anyone, someone must judge, and someone must keep the fund that stands behind everything else. This act is that list, and the list is closed.

- **The remainder is a residue, not a design.** Its thinness is not restraint — it is arithmetic. Take everything the rules and mutuals already govern out of a state, and this is what is left on the table. And it is its own act, not half of a charter, because a residue that presents itself as a co-equal half has already begun to forget that it is a residue.

- **Doubt resolves downward.** Every new problem is presumed to belong to a rule, a mutual, a chamber, a council, or the citizens — the remainder must prove structural impossibility, not convenience, to take it. It is easier to shrink the list than to grow it, by design.

- **The sword points outward.** The military faces the border and nothing else. The one institution that faces inward — the auditors — carries no sword at all: they can only subpoena, charge, and briefly freeze, and a judge stands over each power on a clock, and a free press stands over the whole office.

- **The keeper is not the spender.** The remainder holds the fund of last resort because rescue is a sovereign act — but the fund's ordinary life is automatic, its cap is automatic, its dividend is automatic, and it may never pay its keeper's wages. The one discretionary key is time-boxed and double-locked.

- **The sovereign is funded like a tenant, not a landlord.** No printer, no bonds, no home tax, no reach into the informal economy — the remainder lives on the border and the subsoil, under a cap, with the surplus flowing back to the citizens it serves.

- **The prohibitions outrank the powers, and nothing suspends them.** What the remainder may never do is written as law, not implication — and no war, emergency, or treaty pierces it, with no suspension door held in reserve. The emergency mode can widen a liquidity line; it cannot touch a ballot, a recall, a doctor's door, a printing press, or a cash transaction at a market stall.

Place and trade govern themselves; rules carry the routine; mutuals carry the shocks; the citizens carry the sovereignty. What is left — the border, the indictment, the gavel, and the vault — is the remainder. It is small because everything else is strong.
