## Purpose
Establishes a network of **5,000 member-owned credit unions** (250 per region across 20 regions) that act as New Crossroads' decentralized financial backbone.  

Credit unions issue and manage Bulwark Coin (BWC), deliver reserve-backed Wallets that grow the national metals reserve, provide loans ($452.5 billion in 2025 → $1.2084 trillion by 2075), banking suites ($208 billion), and shares ($94 billion), while allowing every citizen-member to build personal wealth through low-barrier entry, tiered incentives, and ETF-driven reserve growth.  

They self-recapitalize via fees, interest, SWF/SMSWF profits, and **the issuance of special shares (1–2 series per year, $12.5 billion per series)** , support the 65% co-op / 15% corporate / 20% informal GDP split, and ensure broad economic inclusion. The system is designed for **asymmetric scrutiny**: extreme transparency for large transactions and elite actors, complete privacy for ordinary daily transactions.

## Section 1: Core Features & Currency

### 1.1 Dual Currency Role

- **Physical Cash**  
  - Volume (2025): $1.45 trillion (10% of $14.5T GDP), scaling to $4.67 trillion (12% of $38.94T GDP) by 2075  
  - Composition: Gold/silver-flecked polymer notes with holographic security features. Embedded metal content: approx. 5–10% of face value (varying by denomination), providing psychological trust and intrinsic value floor.  
  - Purpose: Primary medium for informal sector transactions (<$100/BWC threshold, indexed to 2025 purchasing power) and all daily private transactions.  
  - Legal Export: **Strictly prohibited**. Cash may not be taken across national borders. Customs enforcement at all ports and land crossings.  
  - Swap Fee: 1% on cash-to-BWC conversions ($14.5 billion/year in 2025, scaling to $46.7 billion/year by 2075)  
  - Privacy: Cash transactions leave no record. Complete anonymity for the user.

- **Bulwark Coin (BWC)**  
  - Type: Blockchain-based digital currency  
  - Reserve: $125–$175 billion (30% gold, 30% palladium, 15% platinum, 10% iridium, 10% rhodium, 5% ruthenium), scaling to $407–$504 billion by 2075  
  - Backing: 0.025 oz precious metal reserve per BWC (~$50/BWC in 2025), scaling to ~$300/BWC by 2075 via ETF returns and SMSWF streaming  
  - Transaction Fees: 0% for transactions under $100; 1% for $100–$10,000; 2% for transactions above $10,000  
  - Transparency Threshold: All BWC transactions above $10,000 are logged in the CWD and subject to audit. Transactions below $10,000 are private and unlogged.  
  - Features: Real-time reserve ratio visible on wallets, no burns, CME-proof via 400 TWh nuclear grid and 10 vaults ($100 million)  
  - Purpose: Facilitates FCL payments, large co-op and corporate transactions, and international trade via CGCI

### 1.2 Self-Recapitalization & Reserve Growth

Credit unions **self-recapitalize** through:  

- **Special Shares Issuance (Primary Mechanism)** : 1–2 series per year, each raising $12.5 billion. Special shares ($1,000–$25,000 per citizen) provide tiered capital that allows credit unions to scale loans from $452.5 billion to $1.2084 trillion by 2075.  
- Transaction, swap, checking, liquidity, micro-loan, and insurance fees  
- Interest on loans (micro 4%, non-SWF 5–6%)  
- SMSWF profits ($2.48 billion/year → $1.736 billion to credit unions)  
- Reserve ETF & SWF investment returns  

Every citizen who buys into a Wallet **grows the national metals reserve** via ETF investments (BWC Reserve ETF + SMSWF ETF), which allocate returns to base backing until ~$300/BWC target, then to personal savings.

**Recapitalization Cycle**:
- Each special shares series raises $12.5 billion in new capital
- At 2 series/year, credit unions raise $25 billion/year in new capital
- This capital is then lent out as co-op, corporate, and informal loans
- Loan interest and fees generate revenue that funds dividends on special shares
- Successful loan performance attracts more special share purchases in subsequent series

**Metal Allocation for Physical Cash**: 40% of SMSWF gold/silver stream is allocated to minting metal flecks for polymer notes, ensuring the cash supply grows organically with mining production.

## Section 3: Shares & Wealth Building

### 3.1 Base Shares
- **Cost**: $1/BWC, max 1,000 ($1,000/BWC lifetime, $94 billion system-wide).  
- **Acquisition**: Buy $100 BWC/year, earn 75% patronage to shares/25% cash, reinvest 4–8% dividends.  
- **Incentives (minors 12–19)**: 1 share for voting in board elections, 1 for town hall attendance.  
- **Mechanics**: Lifetime, non-sellable, estate-transferrable; grants membership, banking, voting.

### 3.2 Special Shares (Citizen-Members Only)

**Issuance Schedule**: 1–2 series per year, at the discretion of credit union boards and Regional Board approval.

**Series Structure**:
- Each series raises $12.5 billion system-wide ($2.5 billion per region, distributed across 250 credit unions/region)
- Series are offered sequentially, not simultaneously, to avoid market saturation
- Between series, credit unions deploy capital from previous series into loans

**Pricing and Terms**:
- **Cost per series**: $1,000–$25,000/BWC per citizen (tiered)
- **Dividends**: 6% for first 3 years, 5–6% thereafter (board-adjusted for inflation >3%)
- **Withdrawals**: $500/BWC/year (1-year cooldown for $1,000/BWC)
- **Insurance**: $50 million insurance pool covers defaults

**Voting Rights**: Non-voting except Gold tier's 0.5 advisory vote on $50 billion non-SWF loans.

**Recapitalization Impact**:
- Each series adds $12.5 billion in new capital to credit union balance sheets
- At 2 series/year, credit unions raise $25 billion/year
- Over 50 years (2025–2075), assuming 2 series/year average, total capital raised = $1.25 trillion
- This enables loan growth from $452.5 billion to $1.2084 trillion

### 3.3 Reserve-Backed Wallet – How It Grows the National Reserve
- **Base Backing**: Starts at 0.025 oz (~$50/BWC).  
- **ETF Mechanism** (BWC Reserve ETF 3–5% + SMSWF ETF 5–7%):  
  - Returns first allocate to increase base backing (target ~$300/BWC over decades).  
  - Once target reached, excess flows to Member Savings Account (personal use).  
- **Citizen Contribution**: Invest up to $5,000–$10,000/BWC per ETF → directly adds physical metals to national reserve via ETF holdings.  
- **Scaling**: From $15.04 billion Wallets (2025) → $16.25 billion+ by 2075; ETF growth pushes reserve from $125–$175 billion → $407–$504 billion.

### 3.4 Privacy and Scrutiny Tiers
- **Wallet balances below $10,000**: Private. No CWD tracking. No reporting requirements.  
- **Wallet balances $10,000–$100,000**: CWD tracks aggregate totals only. No transaction-level detail.  
- **Wallet balances above $100,000**: Automatic audit. Full transaction tracking. Public disclosure of holdings above $1 million.  
- **Cash holdings**: Never tracked. Complete privacy regardless of amount (practically limited by physical constraints).
