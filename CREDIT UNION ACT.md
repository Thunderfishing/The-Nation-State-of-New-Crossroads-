> **Cross-reference**: This act is companion legislation to the *Crossroads Monetary Reform & Economic Stabilization Act of 2025 (Draft 9.0)*. All references to the Daily Living Basket (DLB), Deflation Adjustment Index (DAI), BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR), Informal Economy Guarantee, and Two-Layer Velocity Architecture are governed by that act. Where this act specifies loan rates, they are nominal rates; DAI adjustment is automatic and mandatory per Monetary Act Section 1.6.
>
> **Amendment Note – Draft 5.0**: This draft introduces three major structural changes to enable lending growth while preserving sound money principles: (1) variable metal backing of BWC (25–50% range) with daily public ratio, (2) deposit insurance tied to credit union board voting participation, and (3) Regional Liquidity Pools (RLPs) and a Deposit Insurance Pool (DIP) as lender-of-last-resort and solvency backstops.

## Purpose

Establishes a network of **5,000 member-owned credit unions** (250 per region across 20 regions) that act as New Crossroads' decentralized financial backbone. Credit unions issue and manage Bulwark Coin (BWC), deliver reserve-backed Wallets that grow the national metals reserve, provide DAI-adjusted loans ($452.5 billion in 2025 → $1.2084 trillion by 2075), banking suites ($208 billion), and shares ($94 billion), while allowing every citizen-member to build personal wealth through low-barrier entry, tiered incentives, and ETF-driven reserve growth.

They self-recapitalize via fees, interest, SWF/SMSWF profits, and **the issuance of special shares (1–2 series per year, $12.5 billion per series)**, support the 65% co-op / 15% corporate / 20% informal GDP split, and ensure broad economic inclusion.

The system is designed for **asymmetric scrutiny**: extreme transparency for large transactions and elite actors, complete privacy for ordinary daily transactions. Credit unions are the **formal economy's open door to the informal sector** — they exist to serve cash-based citizens on those citizens' terms, not to pull them into surveillance.

## Section 1: Core Features & Currency

### 1.1 Dual Currency Role

Credit unions operate across both layers of the Two-Layer Velocity Architecture established in the Monetary Act. Their role differs by layer:

**Layer 1 — Physical Cash (Velocity Layer)**

- **Volume (2025)**: $1.45 trillion (10% of $14.5T GDP), scaling to $4.67 trillion (12% of $38.94T GDP) by 2075
- **Composition**: Gold/silver-flecked polymer notes with holographic security features. Embedded metal content: approx. 5–10% of face value (varying by denomination), providing psychological trust and intrinsic value floor.
- **Purpose**: Primary medium for all informal sector transactions at or below the current Daily Living Basket (DLB) threshold and all daily private transactions.
- **Legal Export**: **Strictly prohibited**. Cash may not be taken across national borders.
- **Swap Fee (Cash → BWC)**: **Free**. Credit unions charge no fee for converting cash to BWC. The door into the formal system is always open at no cost.
- **Swap Fee (BWC → Cash)**: 1% ($14.5 billion/year in 2025, scaling to $46.7 billion/year by 2075). Friction is on exit from formal, not entry.
- **Privacy**: Cash transactions leave no record. Credit unions may not log, retain, or report cash transaction data. Complete anonymity.

**Layer 2 — Bulwark Coin (BWC) (Store-of-Value / Formal Layer)**

- **Type**: Blockchain-based digital currency
- **Reserve**: $125–$175 billion (30% gold, 30% palladium, 15% platinum, 10% iridium, 10% rhodium, 5% ruthenium), scaling to $407–$504 billion by 2075
- **Backing Ratio (Variable)**: 
  - **Normal target**: 50% metal backing (0.0125 oz per BWC at $50/BWC)
  - **Minimum permitted**: 25% metal backing (0.00625 oz per BWC)
  - **Crisis floor (Central Council 6/11 vote)**: 25% for up to 12 months, extendable once
  - The remainder (50–75%) is backed by the collective capital of the credit union network, the Deposit Insurance Pool, and the SWF guarantee
- **Published Backing Ratio**: Each credit union must display the **current system-wide metal backing ratio** on every member's wallet interface, updated daily by Treasury. If the ratio falls below 40% for 90 consecutive days, Central Council must convene and publish a remediation plan.
- **Transaction Fees**: 0% under $100 BWC; 1% for $100–$10,000; 1.5% for $10,001–$100,000; 2% above $100,000
- **Transparency Threshold**: All BWC transactions above $10,000 are logged in the CWD and subject to audit. Transactions below $10,000 are private and unlogged.
- **Features**: Real-time reserve ratio visible on wallets, no burns, CME-proof via 400 TWh nuclear grid and 10 vaults ($100 million)
- **Purpose**: Facilitates FCL payments, large co-op and corporate transactions, savings, and international trade via CGCI

**Rationale for variable backing**: A 100%+ over-backed BWC is philosophically pure but prevents lending beyond existing metal stocks. A 25–50% range retains hard backing (never fiat) while allowing credit unions to create money through lending against future repayment. The daily public ratio ensures trust through transparency, not blind faith.

### 1.2 Self-Recapitalization & Reserve Growth

Credit unions **self-recapitalize** through:

- **Special Shares Issuance (Primary Mechanism)**: 1–2 series per year, each raising $12.5 billion. Special shares ($1,000–$25,000 per citizen) provide tiered capital that enables loan growth from $452.5 billion to $1.2084 trillion by 2075.
- Transaction, swap (BWC→Cash only), checking, liquidity, micro-loan, and insurance fees
- Interest on DAI-adjusted loans (micro: 4% nominal / ~1.4% effective; non-SWF: 5–6% nominal / ~2.4–3.4% effective)
- SMSWF profits ($2.48 billion/year → $1.736 billion to credit unions)
- Reserve ETF & SWF investment returns

Every citizen who buys into a Wallet **grows the national metals reserve** via ETF investments (BWC Reserve ETF + SMSWF ETF), which allocate returns to base backing until the ~$300/BWC target is reached, then to personal savings.

**Recapitalization Cycle**:
- Each special shares series raises $12.5 billion in new capital
- At 2 series/year, credit unions raise $25 billion/year in new capital
- Capital is deployed as DAI-adjusted co-op, corporate, and informal loans
- Loan interest and fees generate revenue that funds dividends on special shares
- Successful loan performance attracts more special share purchases in subsequent series

**Metal Allocation for Physical Cash**: 40% of SMSWF gold/silver stream is allocated to minting metal flecks for polymer notes, ensuring the cash supply grows organically with mining production and the velocity layer is always adequately supplied.

### 1.3 Informal Sector Mandate

**Rationale**: The informal economy is not a gap to be closed. It is a protected economic zone. Credit unions are the formal system's handshake with that zone — they must be accessible on informal terms, not formal ones. This mandate is binding on all 5,000 credit unions and all CLS officers.

Credit unions **must**:
- Accept self-attested cash income as sufficient basis for micro-loan approval ($500, 4% nominal / ~1.4% DAI-adjusted effective). No documentation, no income verification, no cash history review.
- Process micro-loan applications within **24 hours** via CLS agents
- Grant one free base share to any member who successfully repays their first micro-loan, as a formal welcome into the credit union system
- Offer a **Basic Wallet** (BWC Layer 2 access) to any citizen without requiring formal income history
- Provide the free Cash→BWC swap at all branch and digital access points
- Post the current DLB threshold, BAR, backing ratio, and DAI-adjusted rate table in all branches (physical and digital), updated each January

Credit unions **must not**:
- Require disclosure of cash transaction history as a condition of any loan, membership, banking service, or government benefit
- Condition micro-loan approval on proof of formal income where cash income is self-attested
- Deny, downgrade, or penalize membership tier status based on a member's ratio of cash to BWC activity
- Use cash withdrawal patterns as evidence of irregular financial behavior absent an independent court order
- Report member cash withdrawal patterns to any government body absent a court order
- Construct or participate in any system designed to track cash transactions at or below the DLB threshold

Violations of the Informal Sector Mandate are subject to Regional Board sanction, CWD audit, and fines of up to $5 million per incident.

## Section 3: Shares & Wealth Building

### 3.1 Base Shares

- **Cost**: $1/BWC, max 1,000 ($1,000/BWC lifetime, $94 billion system-wide).
- **Acquisition**: Buy $100 BWC/year, earn 75% patronage to shares/25% cash, reinvest 4–8% dividends.
- **Informal Sector Welcome Share**: 1 free base share granted automatically on successful repayment of first micro-loan. No purchase required. Grants full membership and voting rights.
- **Incentives (minors 12–19)**: 1 share for voting in board elections, 1 for town hall attendance.
- **Mechanics**: Lifetime, non-sellable, estate-transferrable; grants membership, banking, and voting rights.

### 3.2 Special Shares (Citizen-Members Only)

**Issuance Schedule**: 1–2 series per year, at the discretion of credit union boards and Regional Board approval.

**Series Structure**:
- Each series raises $12.5 billion system-wide ($2.5 billion per region, distributed across 250 credit unions/region)
- Series are offered sequentially, not simultaneously, to avoid market saturation
- Between series, credit unions deploy capital from previous series into DAI-adjusted loans

**Pricing and Terms**:
- **Cost per series**: $1,000–$25,000/BWC per citizen (tiered)
- **Dividends**: 6% for first 3 years, 5–6% thereafter (board-adjusted if inflation exceeds 3%)
- **Withdrawals**: $500/BWC/year (1-year cooldown for $1,000/BWC withdrawal)
- **Insurance**: Backed by the Deposit Insurance Pool (see Section 6.6)

**Voting Rights**: Non-voting except Gold tier's 0.5 advisory vote on $50 billion non-SWF loans.

**Recapitalization Impact**:
- Each series adds $12.5 billion in new capital to credit union balance sheets
- At 2 series/year, credit unions raise $25 billion/year
- Over 50 years (2025–2075), total capital raised = $625 billion–$1.25 trillion
- Enables loan growth from $452.5 billion to $1.2084 trillion

### 3.3 Credit Union Secondary Market (CUSM)

**Rationale**: The $500/BWC/year withdrawal cap on special shares is correct for system stability — it keeps capital deployed in the loan pool. But it creates an individual liquidity problem for members who need to rebalance their holdings. The CUSM resolves this without unlocking capital from the system: shares transfer between members, not out of the credit union network.

- **Eligible participants**: Citizen-members only. Foreign nationals and non-members excluded.
- **Mechanics**: Special shares may be listed and purchased on the CUSM at market-determined prices within a band of 90%–110% of par value. No BWC exits the credit union system — the purchase price moves between member accounts.
- **Price discovery**: Market-determined within the 90%–110% band. Trades above 105% of par require CLS notification (notification only, no approval required).
- **Governance**: CWD logs CUSM aggregate volume only (total shares traded per quarter, system-wide). Individual trades are private between buyer and seller.
- **Effect**: Members needing liquidity may sell shares to willing buyers rather than forcing early withdrawal. Capital remains deployed; individuals retain flexibility.

### 3.4 Reserve-Backed Wallet — How It Grows the National Reserve

- **Base Backing**: Starts at 0.0125 oz (50% of 0.025 oz) per BWC under normal 50% backing. The remaining value is backed by credit union capital + insurance pools.
- **ETF Mechanism** (BWC Reserve ETF 3–5% + SMSWF ETF 5–7%):
  - Returns first allocate to increase metal backing toward the 50% target if below it.
  - Once 50% is achieved, excess flows to Member Savings Account (personal use).
- **Citizen Contribution**: Invest up to $5,000–$10,000/BWC per ETF → directly adds physical metals to national reserve via ETF holdings.
- **Scaling**: From $15.04 billion Wallets (2025) → $16.25 billion+ by 2075; ETF growth pushes reserve from $125–$175 billion → $407–$504 billion.
- **Basic Wallet Access**: Any citizen may open a Basic Wallet without formal income history. Basic Wallets have full Layer 2 access and standard privacy protections. ETF investment requires Base tier share ownership.

### 3.5 Privacy and Scrutiny Tiers

- **Cash holdings**: Never tracked. Complete privacy regardless of amount. Credit unions may not collect, log, or retain data on member cash holdings or cash transactions under any circumstances.
- **Wallet balances below $10,000 BWC**: Private. No CWD tracking. No reporting requirements.
- **Wallet balances $10,000–$100,000 BWC**: CWD tracks aggregate totals only. No transaction-level detail. Annual summary reporting.
- **Wallet balances above $100,000 BWC**: Automatic audit. Full transaction tracking. Public disclosure of holdings above $1 million BWC.

## Section 6: Governance, Oversight & Sustainability

### 6.1 Credit Union Boards

- **Structure**: 5–7 members per credit union, elected every 3 years by member vote (1 member, 1 vote)
- **Informal Sector Seat**: Each credit union board must include at least one member whose primary income is cash-based or informal sector derived, elected by Base tier members. This seat may not be eliminated by board vote.
- **Responsibilities**: Approve loan policies, oversee CLS agents, set local dividend rates within system-wide bands, and ensure compliance with the Informal Sector Mandate (Section 1.3).

### 6.2 Special Shares Oversight

- Each special shares series requires Regional Board approval (6/11 vote) to ensure compliance with the 65/15/20 GDP split and prevent market saturation or manipulation.
- Series are tracked via CWD at the aggregate level. Individual purchases are private below $10,000 BWC.
- The CUSM (Section 3.3) is reported to Regional Boards quarterly in aggregate volume terms only.

### 6.3 Deposit Insurance & Voting Requirement (New)

**Rationale**: Deposit insurance is a mutual benefit of the credit union system. To receive it, members must participate in governance. This ties rights to responsibilities without excluding anyone from basic banking.

**Coverage Limits**:
- **Standard coverage**: Up to $250,000 BWC per member, per credit union
- **Reduced coverage** (non-voting penalty): Up to $50,000 BWC

**Voting Qualification**:
- A member qualifies for standard coverage if they have voted in **at least one of the last three credit union board elections**.
- A member who has missed **two consecutive elections** (without exemption) automatically reverts to reduced coverage ($50,000) until they vote again.

**Grace Period and Exemptions**:
- New members receive **12 months of standard coverage** automatically, regardless of voting history.
- Exemptions (no loss of coverage) granted for:
  - Documented serious illness (self-attested, no verification required for absence under 12 months)
  - Military deployment outside the region
  - Residence outside the region for more than 6 months (e.g., students, temporary workers)
- Exemptions claimed by simple declaration to the credit union. No surveillance, no proof required for absences under 12 months (asymmetric scrutiny applies).

**Reinstatement**:
- A member who votes in any board election after a period of non-voting regains standard coverage within 30 days of the election.

**Relationship to other insurance**:
- The $50 million insurance pool for special shares (Section 3.2) is *supplemental* to deposit insurance. Deposit insurance covers deposits/wallets; the special shares pool covers share defaults.

**Enforcement**:
- Credit unions check voting records automatically via CWD aggregate data (no individual vote disclosure – only a binary "qualified/not qualified" flag).
- Members may appeal a coverage determination to the Regional Board.

### 6.4 Regional Liquidity Pools (RLPs) — Lender of Last Resort (New)

**Rationale**: Credit unions need a lender of last resort, but not a central bank. RLPs are mutual liquidity backstops owned collectively by the credit unions in each region.

**Establishment**:
- Each of the 20 regions establishes a **Regional Liquidity Pool (RLP)**.
- RLPs are administered by the Regional Board, with daily operations by a rotating committee of credit union managers (elected annually).

**Funding**:
1. **Annual contribution**: Each credit union contributes **2% of its total deposits (BWC + cash-equivalent liabilities)** to its regional RLP each year.
   - Contribution is not a fee – it is a *capital stake*. Credit unions retain a proportional claim on the pool.
2. **Patronage diversion**: **20% of each credit union's annual patronage dividends** are automatically directed to the RLP.
   - Example: A credit union with $100M in patronage returns sends $20M to RLP, distributes $80M to members.
3. **Returns**: RLP funds are held in the same asset mix as the national reserve (gold, palladium, platinum, etc.) plus short-term BWC loans to other credit unions in the same region. All investment returns stay in the pool.

**Size target**: Each RLP targets 5–10% of regional deposits.

**Access rules** (borrowing from the RLP):
A credit union may draw from its regional RLP if:
1. Its liquid BWC reserves fall below **10% of deposits** for 5 consecutive business days.
2. It has first attempted to borrow from **at least two other credit unions in the same region** (peer lending first).
3. The Regional Board approves by **6/11 vote**.

**Loan terms from RLP**:
- Interest rate: **BAR + 1%** (above the risk-free appreciation rate)
- Maximum term: **12 months**
- Repayment schedule: Monthly principal + interest
- If a credit union fails to repay, its stake in the RLP is forfeit, and its entire board must stand for re-election within 90 days.

**If the RLP is exhausted**:
- The credit union may petition the **national SWF** as ultimate backstop.
- SWF activation requires **5 Regional Boards jointly certifying an emergency** (5/20 vote).
- SWF loans carry interest at BAR + 2% and require Central Council approval (6/11).

### 6.5 Deposit Insurance Pool (DIP) — Solvency Backstop (New)

**Rationale**: The RLP handles *liquidity* (short-term cash flow). The DIP handles *solvency* (pays depositors if a credit union fails entirely).

**Nesting**: The DIP is nested inside each RLP – each region has its own DIP as a sub-fund of its RLP.

**Funding**:
1. **From patronage**: Of the 20% of patronage dividends sent to the RLP (Section 6.4), **half (10% of total patronage) goes to the DIP** and half to the RLP's liquid facility.
2. **Transaction fee**: **0.1% of every BWC transaction above $10,000** is allocated to the DIP (added to existing BWC fee structure without increasing total fees – the 1.5%/2% tiers absorb this).

**Payout rules**:
- If a credit union becomes insolvent (assets < 80% of liabilities for 90 days), the DIP pays **insured depositors** up to $250,000 BWC (or $50,000 for non-voting members) within 30 days.
- Payouts are made in BWC directly to affected members' wallets at another credit union of their choice.
- The failed credit union's assets are liquidated by the Regional Board, with proceeds returning to the DIP.

**Reinsurance from SWF**:
- If a regional DIP falls below **2% of total insured deposits** in that region, the DIP may draw from the national SWF to restore to 4%.
- SWF reinsurance requires Regional Board approval (11/11 unanimous for that region) plus Central Council confirmation (6/11).

**DIP investment policy**:
- DIP funds are held in the most liquid assets: 50% BWC, 30% short-term government instruments (DLB-indexed bonds), 20% metal.
- DIP may not be invested in loans or illiquid assets.

| Level | Annual Income / Assets | CWD Treatment | Reporting |
|-------|------------------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Informal** | At or below DLB threshold (cash) | Never collected | None. Constitutionally protected. |

| **Elite** | Above $100,000/year or $1M assets | Full transaction tracking | Quarterly audits; public above $10M |
| **Institutional** | Credit unions above $10M revenue | Full transparency | Public annual reports; Regional Board audit |

- **Treasury**: Oversees BWC reserve, $550 billion SWF, $125–$175 billion reserve ETF. Publishes BAR, DAI rate table, and **daily system-wide BWC backing ratio** each morning (including the 0.5% service-sector floor). Provides DLB threshold update to all credit unions by January 15 each year.
- **CWD**: Tracks CLS performance, FCL metrics, aggregate loan data, RLP/DIP aggregate health, and CUSM volume. **Cash transaction data is never collected. Citizen-level BWC data under $10,000 is permanently deleted after 30 days.** Audited by 50–75 auditors + $1 billion blockchain/AI system; fraud cap $2.5 billion; appeals to judiciary.
- **Regional Boards**: 20 boards (11 members each) approve special shares series, major loans, FCL opt-ins, and RLP/DIP operations. Investigate Informal Sector Mandate violations. Annual public reporting on informal sector access metrics and RLP/DIP solvency ratios across all credit unions in their region.
- **Auditors**: 50–75 system auditors. Audit scope explicitly excludes individual cash transaction data — auditors may not request, subpoena, or receive cash transaction records from credit unions or members.

### 6.7 Sustainability

- $50 billion/year SWF projects (greenhouses, CO2 pipelines, parkweb)

- 40% of SMSWF metal stream allocated to physical cash minting, ensuring the velocity layer is always adequately supplied as GDP grows

- RLP and DIP structures ensure self-insurance without a central bank, preserving the anti-fiat, anti-debt character of the system while enabling lending growth

## Appendix C: RLP & DIP Quick Reference for CLS Agents and Credit Union Managers

| Pool | Purpose | Funded by | Used when |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **RLP (Regional Liquidity Pool)** | Short-term liquidity (cash flow) | 2% of deposits annually + 20% of patronage (half to RLP) | Reserves <10% of deposits; peer lending failed |
| **DIP (Deposit Insurance Pool)** | Solvency (pays depositors if CU fails) | 10% of patronage (the other half) + 0.1% on BWC txs >$10k | Credit union insolvent (assets <80% of liabilities) |

**If a credit union needs help**:
1. Borrow from peer credit unions (first, no cost except interest)
2. Borrow from RLP (BAR+1%, 12 months)
3. If RLP exhausted → SWF backstop (requires 5 Regional Boards)

**If a credit union fails**:
1. DIP pays depositors within 30 days (up to $250k/$50k)
2. Regional Board liquidates assets
3. Proceeds return to DIP

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

The Credit Union Act is the operational heart of the Informal Economy Guarantee. Where the Monetary Act declares privacy, the Credit Union Act delivers it.

**Draft 5.0 adds three new pillars** without breaking any existing ones:

1. **Variable backing (25–50% metal)** – This is not fiat. It is *transparent, mutualized sound money*. The remaining 50–75% is backed not by government decree but by the collective capital of 5,000 credit unions, the Deposit Insurance Pool, and the SWF. And you can see the ratio every morning on your wallet. That is more honest than a gold standard that pretends leverage doesn't exist.

2. **Voting-linked deposit insurance** – Insurance is a mutual benefit. Mutual benefits require mutual participation. If you want the full $250k backstop, vote. If you cannot vote (illness, deployment), no penalty. If you choose not to vote, you are still protected up to $50k – enough for any informal saver, but a gentle nudge toward civic engagement.

3. **Regional Liquidity Pools + Deposit Insurance Pool** – The anti-fiat position correctly rejects central banks. But rejecting lender-of-last-resort entirely means accepting that a single bad harvest or a single co-op failure could freeze lending in an entire region. RLPs are *mutual*, not state. They are funded by the credit unions themselves, not by taxes. The SWF is a backstop to the backstop – accessible only when five regions agree, which will almost never happen. That preserves the anti-debt, anti-bailout ethos while adding resilience.

**The informal sector remains untouched.** No RLP contribution is asked of cash-only citizens. No voting requirement applies to those who hold only cash. The micro-loan takes 24 hours. The swap is free. The state still never asks what you spent your cash on.

**The door is open. The system is resilient. And the elite still have nowhere to hide.**

That is the Credit Union Act – Draft 5.0.
