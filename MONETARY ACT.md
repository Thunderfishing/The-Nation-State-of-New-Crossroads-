### 1.5 Daily Living Basket (DLB) â€” Informal Threshold Index

**Rationale**: The threshold below which cash transactions constitute protected informal commerce must track real purchasing power, not nominal values. A $100 threshold that covers a restaurant meal in 2025 should still cover a restaurant meal in 2050. Indexing to a defined basket of common goods ensures the informal sector always covers genuine daily life.

- **Definition**: The DLB is a defined basket of common goods and services representing one week of ordinary household commerce: groceries (for 2 persons, 7 days), one restaurant meal, one tank of fuel, one week of transit fares, and one standard household utility payment.
- **Base Value (2025)**: $100 BWC
- **Adjustment**: Recalculated annually by Treasury statisticians. Published in the first week of January. Automatically updates the cash transaction privacy threshold, BWC 0% fee band, and DLB-referenced loan terms.
- **Governance**: DLB methodology reviewed by Central Council every 5 years (6/11 vote). Methodology is public. Basket composition changes require Regional Board majority (11/20).
- **Effect**: As BWC appreciates, the DLB threshold in BWC terms will generally decline (i.e., $100 in 2025 BWC buys more in 2050 BWC terms). The DLB recalculation counteracts this, keeping the informal layer genuinely covering daily life.

### 1.6 Deflation Adjustment Index (DAI) â€” Loan Rate Correction

**Rationale**: BWC is designed to appreciate approximately 2.6%/year in real terms over the 50-year horizon ($50/BWC â†’ ~$300/BWC by 2075). This is a feature for savers and a potential problem for borrowers: a co-op borrowing 1,000 BWC at 5% nominal faces an effective real borrowing cost of ~7.6% once BWC appreciation is accounted for. Without correction, deflation would systematically discourage investment â€” exactly the wrong outcome for a growing co-op economy. The DAI corrects for this by adjusting the **nominal interest rate** on qualifying loans downward by the published BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR), so that real borrowing costs remain stable and predictable.

#### How the DAI Works

- **BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR)**: Published annually by Treasury in January. Calculated as the trailing 3-year average annual appreciation of BWC's reserve backing (oz per BWC). Smoothed to prevent single-year volatility from distorting loan rates.
- **DAI-Adjusted Rate**: `Nominal Rate âˆ’ BAR = DAI-Adjusted Effective Rate`
  - Example (2025): Nominal rate 5%, BAR 2.6% â†’ DAI-adjusted rate 2.4%
  - The borrower repays in BWC at the nominal rate, but the real burden is calibrated to 2.4%
- **Floor**: DAI-adjusted rates may not fall below 1% regardless of BAR. Prevents zero or negative real rates.
- **Publication**: DAI rate table published annually alongside BAR. All qualifying loan contracts reference the BAR year of origination, locked for the loan term.

## Section 1A: Informal Economy Guarantee

**This section establishes the Informal Economy Guarantee as an affirmative constitutional and legislative protection for cash-based commerce. It is not merely an administrative policy â€” it is a binding prohibition on state surveillance of ordinary economic life.**

### 1A.1 Declaration

The state of New Crossroads declares that:

1. All citizen-to-citizen commerce conducted in physical cash at or below the current Daily Living Basket threshold constitutes **protected informal economic activity**.
2. Citizens engaged in such commerce have an **absolute right to privacy** that no government agency, credit union, or FCL may abridge, compel disclosure of, or use as a condition of access to any service.
3. The state has **no legitimate interest** in tracking, aggregating, or reporting informal sector transactions below the DLB threshold, and affirmatively **prohibits** the construction of any system designed to do so.

### 1A.2 Specific Prohibitions

The following are unlawful for any government body, credit union, CLS officer, FCL, or corporate entity:

- Requiring disclosure of cash transaction history as a condition of any loan, membership, banking service, or government benefit
- Conditioning micro-loan approval on proof of formal income where cash income is self-attested
- Using cash holdings or cash transaction patterns as evidence of tax evasion absent independent corroborating evidence
- Constructing databases, AI systems, or surveillance infrastructure designed to track cash transactions below the DLB threshold
- Refusing service to citizens who decline to disclose cash income

### 1A.3 Credit Union Non-Discrimination Requirement

Credit unions may not:
- Deny membership, downgrade tier status, or withhold dividends based on a member's ratio of cash to BWC activity
- Require BWC transaction history to qualify for micro-loans or informal sector loans
- Report member cash withdrawal patterns to any government body absent a court order

### 1A.4 Informal Sector Micro-Loan Access

Citizens whose primary income is cash-based (informal sector participants) receive automatic access to:
- **Micro-loans** ($500, 4% nominal / ~1.4% DAI-adjusted): Self-attested income, no documentation required. CLS agents process within 24 hours.
- **Base shares** (1 free share on first micro-loan repayment, as a credit union welcome incentive)
- **Basic Wallet** (BWC Layer 2 access without requiring formal income history)

The purpose is to ensure the formal financial system is always accessible to informal sector participants on their terms â€” not to pull them into surveillance.

### 1A.5 Sampling-Only Measurement

The state shall measure the informal sector's contribution to GDP exclusively through:
- Voluntary quarterly household expenditure surveys (anonymized, opt-in)
- Statistical extrapolation from DLB price tracking
- Aggregate cash minting and circulation data (no individual tracking)

No surveillance-based method may be substituted for statistical sampling. The informal sector GDP figure (20% floor) is an estimate, and the legislation accepts this uncertainty as the appropriate price of privacy.

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

The Crossroads monetary system is built on a single principle: **trust through asymmetry**.

The informal economy is not a gap in the system. It is not a problem to be solved, a tax base to be captured, or a population to be graduated into formal finance. It is the **living core of daily economic life** â€” the neighbor who fixes your fence, the market stall, the babysitter, the handshake deal. It is the part of economic life that has always worked best when the state left it alone.

New Crossroads leaves it alone. Not passively â€” actively and legislatively. The prohibition on cash surveillance is not a privacy policy. It is a declaration of what the state is for.

- **Ordinary citizens** face next to no scrutiny. Their cash is private, their small transactions are unlogged, their wallets are their own. The beautiful metal-flecked notes they carry are a daily reminder that the state trusts them â€” and they trust the state in return.

- **The formal system exists to serve the informal**, not replace it. Credit unions offer micro-loans without income verification. CLS agents process applications in 24 hours. The swap into BWC is free. The door is always open â€” and citizens choose when to walk through it.

- **Elites, large co-ops, and corporations** face extreme transparency. Every large transaction is logged. Every major loan is audited. Every FCL profit is disclosed. This prevents the concentration of wealth and power that destroyed the previous system.

- **The DAI ensures deflation is a feature, not a trap.** BWC appreciates because it is backed by real metals. That appreciation benefits savers. The DAI ensures it does not punish co-op borrowers by making the real cost of productive investment invisible and punishing.

- **The state itself** is bound by the same rules. The Central Council cannot print money without reserve backing. The Regional Boards cannot approve loans without public disclosure. The CWD cannot retain citizen data beyond 30 days. And no agency â€” ever â€” may build a system to track what citizens spend their cash on.

Cash is beautiful. BWC is honest. The system is legible. The informal economy is free. And the elite have nowhere to hide.
