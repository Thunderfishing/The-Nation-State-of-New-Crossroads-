The Crossroads Monetary Reform & Economic Stabilization Act of 2025 (Draft 6.7) establishes New Crossroads' monetary system following the 2025 $13 trillion debt reset. It drives a $14.5 trillion GDP (65% co-ops/$9.425 trillion, 15% corporate/$2.175 trillion, 20% informal/$2.9 trillion) for 112 million citizens, targeting $38.94 trillion GDP and $3.082 trillion Sovereign Wealth Fund (SWF) by 2075.  

The system supports 67 million middle-class citizens through a dual currency (physical cash + Bulwark Coin/BWC), 5,000 credit unions, the Crossroads Loan Service (CLS, 60,000 officers), and deep integration with Federated Cooperatives Limited (FCLs). The system is designed for **asymmetric scrutiny**: extreme transparency and oversight for large co-ops, corporations, and elites, while daily transactions for ordinary citizens remain friction-free, private, and low-cost.

## Overview
This act creates:  
- Dual currency (physical cash + blockchain-based BWC) with asymmetric oversight  
- $550 billion SWF scaling to $3.082 trillion  
- $125–$175 billion reserve scaling to $407–$504 billion  
- 94 million Corporate Citizens equipped with $50 BWC wallets (scaling to $326), 1,000 credit union shares (4–8% dividends), and $208 billion banking suite  
- Physical cash designed as the most beautiful currency in the world, with embedded gold/silver flecks for psychological trust and counterfeiting resistance  

Credit unions and CLS maintain the 65/15/20 GDP split, funded by $601.58 billion annual revenue.

## Section 1: Monetary System

### 1.1 Dual Currency System

#### Physical Cash

**Rationale**: Cash serves as the everyday medium of exchange for 130 million citizens, particularly for the $7.8 trillion informal sector (20% of GDP by 2075). It must be trusted, beautiful, hard to counterfeit, and legally confined to the domestic economy. Unlike digital BWC, cash transactions leave no trace in the CWD — providing privacy for ordinary citizens while preventing elite capital flight.

- **Volume (2025)**: $1.45 trillion (10% of $14.5T GDP), scaling to $4.67 trillion (12% of $38.94T GDP) by 2075  
- **Composition**: Polymer substrate with embedded gold and silver flecks (approx. 5–10% of face value by metal content, varying by denomination)  
- **Design**: Holographic security features, regional artistic motifs (e.g., Corridon barges, Frostpeak mountains, Ember Range geothermal fields), ranked among the world's most beautiful currencies  
- **Backing**: No separate reserve. The embedded metal flecks provide psychological trust and intrinsic value floor. Full backing comes from convertibility to BWC (see below) and state acceptance for taxes and fees.  
- **Denominations**: $1, $5, $10, $20, $50, $100 BWC (indexed to 2025 purchasing power, adjusted biennially for 3% GDP growth by Central Council, 6/11 vote)  
- **Swap Fee**: 1% on cash-to-BWC conversions ($14.5 billion/year in 2025, scaling to $46.7 billion/year by 2075)  
- **Legal Export**: **Strictly prohibited**. Physical cash may not be taken across national borders. Customs enforcement at all ports (97 million tons capacity, Regions 12, 18) and land crossings. Violations subject to seizure and fines up to 10x face value.  
- **Counterfeiting Resistance**: Embedded metal flecks require actual precious metal; polymer substrate requires industrial-scale equipment; holographic features require optical expertise. The barrier to entry is so high that only state actors could counterfeit, and the state has no incentive.  
- **Cost**: $500 million/year minting and distribution (0.034% of cash volume), offset by swap fees

#### Bulwark Coin (BWC)

**Rationale**: BWC serves as the store of value and formal economy medium. It is fully backed by the reserve, deflationary by design, and fully transparent. Large transactions (above $10,000) are tracked in the CWD to prevent elite tax evasion, money laundering, and capital flight — while small transactions remain private.

- **Type**: Blockchain-based digital currency  
- **Reserve**: $125–$175 billion (30% gold, 30% palladium, 15% platinum, 10% iridium, 10% rhodium, 5% ruthenium), scaling to $407–$504 billion by 2075  
- **Backing Ratio**: 100%+ (over-backed, deflationary)  
- **Transaction Fees**: 0% for transactions under $100; 1% for transactions $100–$10,000; 2% for transactions above $10,000 (progressive fee structure incentivizes cash for small purchases and discourages large-scale elite avoidance)  
- **Transparency Threshold**: All BWC transactions above $10,000 are logged in the CWD and subject to audit. Transactions below $10,000 are private and unlogged.  
- **Features**: Real-time reserve ratio visible on wallets, no burns, CME-proof via 400 TWh nuclear grid and 10 vaults ($100 million)  
- **Purpose**: Facilitates FCL payments (~$112,000/year per participant), large co-op and corporate transactions, and international trade via CGCI

**Rationale**: The PMI provides real-time tracking of the 65/15/20 GDP balance, allowing early intervention before co-op GDP dips below 60%. Adjustments are automatic and transparent, visible on every citizen's wallet.

## Section 5: Governance & Oversight

**Rationale**: Oversight is layered and redundant. Large transactions and elite actors face full transparency; ordinary citizens face next to none. The CWD tracks only what is necessary to prevent fraud and capital flight.

- **Bodies**: 11-member Central Council, 20 Regional Boards (220 members: 200 masters, 20 wildcards), 10 National Assembly grandmasters, judiciary.  
- **Asymmetric Oversight Architecture**:
  - **Citizen Level (Under $10,000/year)**: No CWD tracking. No transaction fees for cash. No reporting requirements. Complete privacy.
  - **Middle Level ($10,000–$100,000/year)**: CWD tracks aggregate totals only. No transaction-level detail. Annual summary reporting.
  - **Elite Level (Above $100,000/year or $1M assets)**: Full CWD transaction tracking. Quarterly audits. Public disclosure of holdings above $10M. Subject to SAP review if irregularities found.
  - **Institutional Level (FCLs, corporations, credit unions above $10M revenue)**: Full transparency. Public annual reports. Quarterly audits. Regional Board oversight. Central Council review for major decisions.
- **Treasury**: Oversees BWC, $550 billion SWF, $125–$175 billion reserve ETF ($1.25 billion/year).  
- **CWD**: $150 million/year tracks CLS performance, student venture loans, FCL metrics, citizen investments ($2,600 wallet, $500,000 loan). **Citizen-level data is anonymized and aggregated; individual transaction data for amounts under $10,000 is permanently deleted after 30 days.** Audited by 50–75 auditors + $1 billion blockchain/AI, capping $2.5 billion fraud; appeals to judiciary.

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

The Crossroads monetary system is built on a single principle: **trust through asymmetry**.

- **Ordinary citizens** face next to no scrutiny. Their cash is private, their small transactions are unlogged, their wallets are their own. The beautiful metal-flecked notes they carry are a daily reminder that the state trusts them — and they trust the state in return.

- **Elites, large co-ops, and corporations** face extreme transparency. Every large transaction is logged. Every major loan is audited. Every FCL profit is disclosed. This prevents the concentration of wealth and power that destroyed the previous system.

- **The state itself** is bound by the same rules. The Central Council cannot print money without reserve backing. The Regional Boards cannot approve loans without public disclosure. The auditors cannot hide fraud. The CWD cannot retain citizen data beyond 30 days.

The result is a system that is simultaneously:
- **Free** for the 130 million citizens going about their daily lives
- **Transparent** for the 27,100 FCLs and 5,000 credit unions building the co-op economy
- **Accountable** for the 220 Regional Board members, 11 Central Council members, and 10 Assembly grandmasters governing the nation

Cash is beautiful. BWC is honest. The system is legible. And the elite have nowhere to hide.
