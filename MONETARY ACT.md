## Section 1: Monetary System

### 1.1 Dual Currency System — Two-Layer Velocity Architecture

New Crossroads operates an intentional **two-layer monetary architecture**:

- **Layer 1 — Cash (Velocity Layer)**: High-frequency, private, friction-free. Handles all daily citizen commerce. Designed for maximum turnover. No surveillance, no fees, no records.
- **Layer 2 — BWC (Store-of-Value / Formal Layer)**: Lower-frequency, transparent above $10,000, deflationary by design. Handles institutional transactions, large purchases, savings, and international trade.

These layers are complementary, not competing. Together they capture the full range of economic activity without forcing any citizen into the surveillance architecture to buy groceries.

#### Physical Cash — Layer 1

**Rationale**: Cash is the velocity engine of the New Crossroads economy. It exists to be spent, passed hand to hand, and spent again. The state's goal is not to track it but to ensure there is always enough of it, that it is trusted and beautiful, and that no citizen is ever penalized for using it. The informal sector — all commerce conducted in cash below the Daily Living Basket threshold — is a protected constitutional economic zone.

- **Volume (2025)**: $1.45 trillion (10% of $14.5T GDP), scaling to $4.67 trillion (12% of $38.94T GDP) by 2075
- **Composition**: Polymer substrate with embedded gold and silver flecks (approx. 5–10% of face value by metal content, varying by denomination)
- **Design**: Holographic security features, regional artistic motifs (e.g., Corridon barges, Frostpeak mountains, Ember Range geothermal fields), ranked among the world's most beautiful currencies
- **Backing**: Embedded metal flecks provide psychological trust and intrinsic value floor. Full backing via convertibility to BWC and state acceptance for taxes and fees.
- **Denominations**: $1, $5, $10, $20, $50, $100 BWC — adjusted biennially to the **Daily Living Basket (DLB)** index (see Section 1.5) by Central Council (6/11 vote), ensuring the cash layer always covers genuine daily commerce
- **Swap Fee (Cash → BWC)**: **Free**. No fee to enter the formal system from cash.
- **Swap Fee (BWC → Cash)**: 1% ($14.5 billion/year in 2025, scaling to $46.7 billion/year by 2075). Encourages daily spending to remain in cash; the friction is on exit from formal, not entry.
- **Legal Export**: **Strictly prohibited**. Physical cash may not be taken across national borders. Customs enforcement at all ports (97 million tons capacity, Regions 12, 18) and land crossings. Violations subject to seizure and fines up to 10x face value.
- **Counterfeiting Resistance**: Embedded metal flecks require actual precious metal; polymer substrate requires industrial-scale equipment; holographic features require optical expertise. The barrier to entry is so high that only state actors could counterfeit, and the state has no incentive.
- **Cost**: $500 million/year minting and distribution (0.034% of cash volume), offset by BWC-to-cash swap fees

#### Bulwark Coin (BWC) — Layer 2

**Rationale**: BWC is the store of value and formal economy medium. It is backed by a hybrid of physical metal and the collective capital of the credit union network, making it deflationary by design but not over-backed to the point of preventing lending. Because BWC appreciates in real terms (target ~2.6%/year from $50/BWC to ~$300/BWC by 2075), borrowing in BWC carries an implicit real cost above the nominal interest rate. The **Deflation Adjustment Index (DAI)** (see Section 1.6) corrects for this on all qualifying loans, ensuring the deflationary design does not choke investment or punish co-ops.

The backing ratio is **variable** – a deliberate departure from 100%+ over-backing. This is not fiat. The remaining 50–75% is backed by the mutual capital of 5,000 credit unions, the Deposit Insurance Pool, and the SWF guarantee. The ratio is published daily on every citizen's wallet.

- **Type**: Blockchain-based digital currency
- **Reserve**: $125–$175 billion (30% gold, 30% palladium, 15% platinum, 10% iridium, 10% rhodium, 5% ruthenium), scaling to $407–$504 billion by 2075
- **Backing Ratio (Variable)**:
  - **Normal target**: 50% metal backing (0.0125 oz per BWC at $50/BWC)
  - **Minimum permitted**: 25% metal backing (0.00625 oz per BWC)
  - **Crisis floor (Central Council 6/11 vote)**: 25% for up to 12 months, extendable once
  - The remainder (50–75%) is backed by the collective capital of the credit union network, the Deposit Insurance Pool (see Credit Union Act Section 6.5), and the SWF guarantee
- **Published Backing Ratio**: Treasury publishes the **system-wide metal backing ratio** daily at 9:00 AM. Each citizen's wallet displays the current ratio. If the ratio falls below 40% for 90 consecutive days, Central Council must convene and publish a remediation plan within 30 days.
- **Appreciation Rate**: ~2.6%/year average target (0.025 oz → ~0.0875 oz backing per BWC by 2075 under a 100% regime; under variable backing, metal content per BWC may rise more slowly as lending expands); published annually as the **BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR)** by Treasury, calculated as the trailing 3-year average appreciation of BWC's *effective purchasing power* (combination of metal backing + credit union capital growth)
- **Transaction Fees**:
  - 0% for transactions under $100 BWC
  - 1% for transactions $100–$10,000 BWC
  - 1.5% for transactions $10,001–$100,000 BWC *(smoothed from prior 2% cliff to reduce bunching behavior)*
  - 2% for transactions above $100,000 BWC
- **Transparency Threshold**: All BWC transactions above $10,000 are logged in the CWD and subject to audit. Transactions below $10,000 are private and unlogged.
- **Features**: Real-time reserve ratio visible on wallets, no burns, CME-proof via 400 TWh nuclear grid and 10 vaults ($100 million)
- **Purpose**: Facilitates FCL payments (~$112,000/year per participant), large co-op and corporate transactions, and international trade via CGCI

**Why variable backing?** A 100%+ over-backed BWC is philosophically pure but economically rigid – every BWC lent must first exist as metal. The 25–50% range preserves hard backing (never falling to fiat's 0%) while allowing credit unions to create money through lending against future repayment. The daily public ratio ensures trust through transparency, not blind faith.

### 1.5 Daily Living Basket (DLB) — Informal Threshold Index

**Rationale**: The threshold below which cash transactions constitute protected informal commerce must track real purchasing power, not nominal values. A $100 threshold that covers a restaurant meal in 2025 should still cover a restaurant meal in 2050. Indexing to a defined basket of common goods ensures the informal sector always covers genuine daily life.

- **Definition**: The DLB is a defined basket of common goods and services representing one week of ordinary household commerce: groceries (for 2 persons, 7 days), one restaurant meal, one tank of fuel, one week of transit fares, and one standard household utility payment.
- **Base Value (2025)**: $100 BWC
- **Adjustment**: Recalculated annually by Treasury statisticians. Published in the first week of January. Automatically updates the cash transaction privacy threshold, BWC 0% fee band, and DLB-referenced loan terms.
- **Governance**: DLB methodology reviewed by Central Council every 5 years (6/11 vote). Methodology is public. Basket composition changes require Regional Board majority (11/20).
- **Effect**: As BWC appreciates, the DLB threshold in BWC terms will generally decline (i.e., $100 in 2025 BWC buys more in 2050 BWC terms). The DLB recalculation counteracts this, keeping the informal layer genuinely covering daily life.

### 1.6 Deflation Adjustment Index (DAI) — Loan Rate Correction

**Rationale**: BWC is designed to appreciate approximately 2.6%/year in real terms over the 50-year horizon ($50/BWC → ~$300/BWC by 2075 under a 100% backing regime; under variable backing, appreciation may be slightly moderated by lending growth, but the DAI remains necessary). This is a feature for savers and a potential problem for borrowers: a co-op borrowing 1,000 BWC at 5% nominal faces an effective real borrowing cost of ~7.6% once BWC appreciation is accounted for. Without correction, deflation would systematically discourage investment — exactly the wrong outcome for a growing co-op economy. The DAI corrects for this by adjusting the **nominal interest rate** on qualifying loans downward by the published BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR), so that real borrowing costs remain stable and predictable.

#### How the DAI Works

- **BWC Appreciation Rate (BAR)**: Published annually by Treasury in January. Calculated as the trailing 3-year average annual appreciation of BWC's *effective purchasing power* (combination of metal backing growth + credit union capital growth + reserve ETF returns). Smoothed to prevent single-year volatility from distorting loan rates.
- **DAI-Adjusted Rate**: `Nominal Rate − BAR = DAI-Adjusted Effective Rate`
  - Example (2025): Nominal rate 5%, BAR 2.6% → DAI-adjusted rate 2.4%
  - The borrower repays in BWC at the nominal rate, but the real burden is calibrated to 2.4%
- **Floor**: DAI-adjusted rates may not fall below 1% for standard loans, **or 0.5% for service-sector loans** (healthcare, education, infrastructure), regardless of BAR. Prevents zero or negative real rates while acknowledging the higher labor subsidy in service sectors.
- **Publication**: DAI rate table published annually alongside BAR. All qualifying loan contracts reference the BAR year of origination, locked for the loan term.

|---|---|---|
| **Co-ops (all FCLs)** | Mandatory | 1.0% |
| **Credit Union non-SWF loans** | Mandatory | 1.0% |
| **Micro-loans** | Mandatory | 0.5% |
| **Informal sector loans** | Mandatory | 0.5% |
| **Service-sector loans (healthcare, education, infrastructure)** | Mandatory | 0.5% |
| **Corporate loans** | Optional | 1.0% |
| **Special shares** | Not applicable | — |

#### Secondary Market for Special Shares

To improve liquidity without unlocking the formal capital base, citizen-members may trade special shares on an internal **Credit Union Secondary Market (CUSM)**:
- Trades permitted between citizen-members only. Foreign nationals and non-members excluded.
- No cash-out: shares transfer between member accounts; no BWC is extracted from the credit union system.
- Price discovery: market-determined within a band of 90%–110% of par value. Trades above par require CLS notification (no approval needed, notification only).
- Governance: CWD logs secondary market aggregate volume only. Individual trades are private.
- Effect: Citizens who need liquidity can sell shares to willing buyers rather than forcing early withdrawal ($500/BWC/year cap), keeping capital deployed in the loan system while giving individuals flexibility.

#### Circuit Breaker for Rapid BWC Appreciation

If BAR exceeds 3% for three consecutive years, the Treasury shall automatically:
- Reduce all DAI floors to 0.25% for the following year
- Publish a public notice of "Deflation Caution" with recommended borrowing incentives
- Suspend the 1% BWC→Cash swap fee for 12 months to encourage liquidity

This circuit breaker prevents deflation from unexpectedly choking investment during periods of rapid currency appreciation.

## Section 1A: Informal Economy Guarantee

**This section establishes the Informal Economy Guarantee as an affirmative constitutional and legislative protection for cash-based commerce. It is not merely an administrative policy — it is a binding prohibition on state surveillance of ordinary economic life.**

### 1A.1 Declaration

The state of New Crossroads declares that:

1. All citizen-to-citizen commerce conducted in physical cash at or below the current Daily Living Basket threshold constitutes **protected informal economic activity**.
2. Citizens engaged in such commerce have an **absolute right to privacy** that no government agency, credit union, or FCL may abridge, compel disclosure of, or use as a condition of access to any service.
3. The state has **no legitimate interest** in tracking, aggregating, or reporting informal sector transactions below the DLB threshold, and affirmatively **prohibits** the construction of any system designed to do so.

### 1A.2 Specific Prohibitions

The following are unlawful for any government body, credit union, CLS officer, FCL, or corporate entity:

- Requiring disclosure of cash transaction history as a condition of any loan, membership, banking service, or government benefit
- Conditioning micro-loan approval on proof of formal income where cash income is self-attested
- Using cash holdings or cash transaction patterns as evidence of tax evasion absent independent corroborating evidence
- Constructing databases, AI systems, or surveillance infrastructure designed to track cash transactions below the DLB threshold
- Refusing service to citizens who decline to disclose cash income

### 1A.3 Credit Union Non-Discrimination Requirement

Credit unions may not:
- Deny membership, downgrade tier status, or withhold dividends based on a member's ratio of cash to BWC activity
- Require BWC transaction history to qualify for micro-loans or informal sector loans
- Report member cash withdrawal patterns to any government body absent a court order

### 1A.4 Informal Sector Micro-Loan Access

Citizens whose primary income is cash-based (informal sector participants) receive automatic access to:
- **Micro-loans** ($500, 4% nominal / ~1.4% DAI-adjusted): Self-attested income, no documentation required. CLS agents process within 24 hours.
- **Base shares** (1 free share on first micro-loan repayment, as a credit union welcome incentive)
- **Basic Wallet** (BWC Layer 2 access without requiring formal income history)

The purpose is to ensure the formal financial system is always accessible to informal sector participants on their terms — not to pull them into surveillance.

### 1A.5 Sampling-Only Measurement

The state shall measure the informal sector's contribution to GDP exclusively through:
- Voluntary quarterly household expenditure surveys (anonymized, opt-in)
- Statistical extrapolation from DLB price tracking
- Aggregate cash minting and circulation data (no individual tracking)

No surveillance-based method may be substituted for statistical sampling. The informal sector GDP figure (20% floor) is an estimate, and the legislation accepts this uncertainty as the appropriate price of privacy.

| 14–16% | Reduce all SWF contribution rates by 50% |
| >16% | Suspend all SWF contributions entirely until ratio falls below 12% |

**Citizen Dividend Mechanics**:
- Calculated annually by Treasury on January 15
- Paid in April (50% physical cash, 50% BWC)
- Cash portion is untracked and constitutionally protected
- BWC portion is deposited directly into citizen wallets
- No tax liability on dividends

**Contribution Rate Cuts** apply proportionally to:
- Financial transactions tax (rate reduced from 0.3% to 0.225%, 0.15%, or 0%)
- Corporate SWF contribution (rate reduced from 5% to 3.75%, 2.5%, or 0%)
- Resource surcharge (rate reduced from 3% to 2.25%, 1.5%, or 0%)

### 2.4 Contribution Phase-Out

By 2075–2100, the SWF is designed to **wean itself off contributions**:

| Phase | Years | Annual contribution | SWF growth driver |
|-------|-------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Build | 2025–2050 | $362.5B | Contributions + returns |
| Stabilize | 2050–2075 | $150B | Mostly returns |
| Mature | 2075–2100 | $0 | Returns only |
| Dividend | 2100+ | Negative (distributions exceed returns during booms) | Smoothing only |

At maturity, the SWF acts solely as a **buffer** — growing in bad years (when returns are negative and no dividends paid), shrinking in good years (when dividends distribute excess). It never balloons.

## Section 5: Governance & Oversight

**Rationale**: Oversight is layered and redundant. Large transactions and elite actors face full transparency; ordinary citizens face next to none. - **Asymmetric Oversight Architecture**:

## Design Philosophy (Informative)

The Crossroads monetary system is built on a single principle: **trust through asymmetry**.

The informal economy is not a gap in the system. It is not a problem to be solved, a tax base to be captured, or a population to be graduated into formal finance. It is the **living core of daily economic life** — the neighbor who fixes your fence, the market stall, the babysitter, the handshake deal. It is the part of economic life that has always worked best when the state left it alone.

New Crossroads leaves it alone. Not passively — actively and legislatively. The prohibition on cash surveillance is not a privacy policy. It is a declaration of what the state is for.

- **Ordinary citizens** face next to no scrutiny. Their cash is private, their small transactions are unlogged, their wallets are their own. The beautiful metal-flecked notes they carry are a daily reminder that the state trusts them — and they trust the state in return.

- **The formal system exists to serve the informal**, not replace it. Credit unions offer micro-loans without income verification. CLS agents process applications in 24 hours. The swap into BWC is free. The door is always open — and citizens choose when to walk through it.

- **Elites, large co-ops, and corporations** face extreme transparency. Every large transaction is logged. Every major loan is audited. Every FCL profit is disclosed. This prevents the concentration of wealth and power that destroyed the previous system.

- **The DAI ensures deflation is a feature, not a trap.** BWC appreciates because it is backed by real metals and mutual capital. That appreciation benefits savers. The DAI ensures it does not punish co-op borrowers by making the real cost of productive investment invisible and punishing. The new 0.5% floor for service sectors recognizes that healthcare, education, and infrastructure workers receive substantial non-monetary compensation through the Education Act's labor subsidy.

- **Variable backing (25–50%) is not a compromise with fiat.** It is an honest acknowledgment that all money systems involve trust. The gold standard pretended that 100% backing eliminated trust – but it only hid the trust in the banking system's leverage. New Crossroads puts the trust in plain sight: 50% metal, 50% mutual capital. You see the ratio every day. That is more honest than a system that pretends leverage doesn't exist.

- **The SWF is designed to be self-limiting.** It will never grow beyond 12% of GDP. Automatic stabilizers reduce contributions when it approaches the cap. Citizen dividends return excess wealth directly to the people who own it — in cash, untracked, constitutionally protected. By 2100, the SWF lives entirely off returns, with contributions phased to zero.

- **The state itself** is bound by the same rules. The Central Council cannot print money without reserve backing. The Regional Boards cannot approve loans without public disclosure. The CWD cannot retain citizen data beyond 30 days. And no agency — ever — may build a system to track what citizens spend their cash on.

Cash is beautiful. BWC is honest. The backing ratio is transparent. The system is legible. The informal economy is free. The SWF is capped. And the elite have nowhere to hide.
