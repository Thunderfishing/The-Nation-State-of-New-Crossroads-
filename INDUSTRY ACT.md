## Section 0.5: Legal Status & Transitions 

All industrial activities under this Act must respect the **Natural Person vs Legal Person** distinction from the *Auto-Incorporation vs Business Registration Flowchart* (Appendix B):

- **Natural Persons** (citizens ≥20, auto-incorporated):
  - May engage in **informal sector** industrial activities (cash only, no hiring, no limited liability, no taxes if <$100k revenue/year).
  - Informal activities include: small-scale hemp crafting, artisanal jewelry (<$100k/year), direct-to-consumer wood products, CO2 credit trading (≤$100k/year), small-batch biofuels, hand tools.
  - No business license required. Protected under Sec 1A of the *Auto-Incorporation Framework*.
  - May transition to **Co-op** at any time with no penalty and free Cash→BWC swap.

- **Legal Persons** (Co-ops or Corporations):
  - Required for: hiring employees, limited liability, revenues >$100k/year, export contracts, foreign investment partnerships, **heavy industry** (see Heavy Industry Clause below).
  - Must register as **Co-op** first (FCL status). Corporation registration is **discouraged** (higher taxes: 15–20% corporate vs 5% co-op profit tax; 15% GDP cap on corporate sector).

- **Transitions** (penalty-free only as shown in flowchart):
  - **Informal → Co-op**: Free, no penalty, unlimited Cash→BWC swap allowed via credit unions.
  - **Co-op → Corporation**: Discouraged. Requires NIB approval (8/11 vote), triggers 15–20% corporate tax (up from 5% co-op profit tax), and enforces 15% GDP cap on corporate sector. Co-op members lose FCL voting rights.

- **Heavy Industry Clause**:
  - The following sectors **cannot be operated by Natural Persons** in the informal sector due to liability, scale, or export complexity:
    - Steel production (>1,000 tons/year)
    - Aerospace & military/missile technology
    - Nuclear technology (SMR production)
    - Shipbuilding (>1 vessel/year)
    - CO2 industry (pipelines, aggregates, fuels, >$100k/year)
    - Foreign factory ownership or partnership
    - Heavy equipment manufacturing (excavators, tractors, combines, >$100k/year)
    - Large-scale hemp processing (>100 tons/year)
    - Transformer production (>100 units/year)
  - These sectors require **Co-op registration as minimum**. Corporation allowed only under discouraged transition (NIB exception required).

- **Penalties for Structural Violations**:
  - Any Natural Person or Corporation operating heavy industry without proper Co-op registration shall:
    1. Pay back taxes at corporate rate (20%) for all past revenue.
    2. Forfeit eligibility for SWF loans for 5 years.
    3. Be subject to mandatory conversion to Co-op within 180 days, or face dissolution of business license.
  - Enforcement by NIB and Central Council, tracked via Crossroads Workforce Database (CWD).
